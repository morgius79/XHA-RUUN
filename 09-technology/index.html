Chapter 9: Xha'Ruun Technology

"We do not fly faster than light. We do not resurrect the dead. We do not read minds. But we have learned to ignite a star in a reactor, build bridges to the sky, and speak across the entire system. Is that enough? For now — yes." — Fusion engineer Thar-Vek-Sha, "Energy and Civilization," Era of Unity

Related sections: → see [Ch.01, "Fundamental Limits"], → see [Ch.02, "Star System"], → see [Ch.07, "History"] Volume: 55 pages

Introduction

The technological level of Xha'Ruun civilization can be characterized as ~0.78 on the Kardashev scale — they are in the transition stage from a planetary to a stellar civilization. They have mastered thermonuclear fusion, built an orbital elevator, colonized the nearest bodies of the Khar'Vex system — but still depend on their home planet for most resources.

A key feature of Xha'Ruun technological development is acceptance of fundamental limitations. Faster-than-light drives, time travel, full-fledged artificial intelligence — all are recognized as physically or ethically impossible. Xha'Ruun technologies develop not outward (violating the laws of physics), but inward (maximally efficiently using what is permitted).

Fuel base:

  • Deuterium: abundant (→ see [Ch.01, "Nucleosynthesis"]) — concentration 3.2×

  • Helium-3: mined from the surface of the moon Thel, also synthesized in reactors

9.1.3 Solar Power

Due to lower insolation (→ see [Ch.02, "Khar'Vex"]), solar panels on Thexar occupy larger areas. However, the high efficiency of chromophyll photocells (→ see [Ch.04, "Photosynthesis"]) partially compensates for this.

9.2 Materials Science

9.2.1 Superheavy Elements

KEY FACT

Due to the elevated strong interaction constant (αₛ = 0.1197), superheavy elements with Z = 126–130 are stable in the Xha'Ruun universe. Xha'Ruun synthesize and use them on an industrial scale.

Element Z A (stable) Half-life Properties Application
Kharinium (Kh) 127 327 12,400 y Ferromagnet, high T_c Superconductors
Vexanium (Vx) 128 330 8,200 y High density (23.3 kh/dl³) Ballast, shielding
Tharinium (Thr) 129 333 3,500 y Refractory (T_melt = 2,180.4 °X) Fusion reactors
Nurinium (Nur) 130 336 1,800 y Catalytic activity Chemical industry

IMPORTANT

Superheavy elements are a key technological advantage of Xha'Ruun. Without kharinium superconductors, the orbital elevator would be impossible (the required material strength cannot be achieved with ordinary alloys). Without tharinium lining, long-lived fusion reactors would not exist.

9.2.2 Nanotubes and Graphene

Carbon nanotubes and graphene are produced on an industrial scale. Used for:

  • Orbital elevator cable — carbon nanotubes + kharinium doping (tensile strength: 1,150.8 thou-atmo)
  • Composite materials for aviation and space
  • Electronics (graphene transistors)

9.2.3 Biomimetic Materials

Inspired by Thexar nature (→ see [Ch.04, "Biochemistry"]):

  • Si-channel membranes — for water and gas filtration
  • Selenium polymers — wear-resistant coatings
  • Silicon-organic composites — lightweight and strong (analog of coral, but synthetic)

The orbital elevator is an engineering feat of Xha'Ruun civilization. It connects the surface of Thexar to geostationary orbit (GSO), allowing cargo delivery to space with negligible energy costs compared to rocket launch.

Elevator construction:

Component Description
Anchor (surface) Hexagonal platform 0.2 × 0.2 mo, at 2,926.8 sha above sea level (equatorial zone)
Cable Bundle of carbon nanotubes doped with kharinium. Diameter: 14.0 pa at base, 0.6 pa at orbital end
Cabins Electromagnetic lifts, 4 cabins (2 ascending, 2 descending simultaneously)
Counterweight GSO station "GSO-1" (expanded, mass ~2,545.5 ru)
Energy Solar panels on cable + laser energy transmission from the surface

Elevator economics:

  • Cargo delivery cost to orbit: ~200 VT/kg (vs. ~12,000 VT/kg by rocket)
  • Payback period: achieved in 80 y
  • Passenger traffic: ~5,000 persons/y (specialists, tourists)
  • Cargo flow: ~1,454.5 ru/y (station materials, spacecraft)

Selenium therapy (antioxidants) +1,500 +15 years

9.5.2 Regenerative Medicine

Xha'Ruun possess the technology of stimulating limb regeneration — a lost limb (except vexes) can be regrown in 6–12 months. The technology is based on activation of embryonic stem cells and bio-implants from silicon-organic matrices.

9.5.3 Genetics

Genetic modification of somatic cells is legal and widely used for treating hereditary diseases. Modification of the germline (heritable changes) is prohibited by the ethical code of the Healers guild.

IMPORTANT

Xha'Ruun fundamentally do not engage in eugenics or species improvement through genetics. The Red Rot epidemic (−2,120 y.c.) left a deep mark on the collective memory: past attempts to "improve" the population through selection led to catastrophes. The modern position: "We are good enough as we are."

The "Nur-Vex" probe is the first artificial object of Xha'Ruun to leave the star system. Its mission is to fly past the nearest star (red dwarf M3.5V, no habitable planets) and transmit data.

Technical details of the probe:

System Characteristic
Engine Photon sail (area: ~1,487 sha²) + laser boost from Thexar orbit (power: 100 GW, duration: 20 min)
Hull Silicon-organic composite, thickness 0.23 pa. Shielding: vexanium foil (0.01 pa) against cosmic dust
Power RTG based on kharinium (half-life 12,400 y) + solar panels
Communications Laser channel (100 Mbit/s at up to 1 ly, 10 Mbit/s at 5 ly)
Signal delay ~5.2 y one way (at maximum distance)
Scientific instruments Spectrometer (IR+UV), high-resolution camera, magnetometer, dust detector

Probe mission:

  1. Flyby of the red dwarf (+3,865 y.c.) — surface imaging, spectroscopy of planetary atmospheres
  2. Measurement of the interstellar medium and magnetic field beyond the heliosphere
  3. Data transmission — an estimated ~3 TB of scientific information over 10 y of active phase
  4. After the mission concludes — the probe will continue moving toward the next star (0.03 ly per 1,000 y)

KEY FACT

The "Nur-Vex" probe moves so fast (0.08 c) that it will reach the nearest star in ~65 y. But due to time dilation (special relativity), 0.2 y less will pass on the probe than on Thexar. For Xha'Ruun, this is the first experiment with relativistic effects at interstellar distances.

9.7 Planetary Defense

9.7.1 Detection System

Xha'Ruun maintain a network of telescopes and radars for detecting potentially hazardous asteroids and comets:

Component Coverage Sensitivity
Ground telescopes (14) Full sky Objects >61.0 sha
Space telescope "Thar-Ven" L2 point Objects >12.2 sha
Orbital radar (equator) Inner system Objects >6.1 sha

9.7.2 Deflection System

Upon detecting a hazardous object (collision probability >1%), the following methods are applied:

  1. Small objects (<122.0 sha): laser surface vaporization (ablation)
  2. Medium objects (122.0–609.8 sha): kinetic impactor (mass 6.1–12.1 ru)
  3. Large objects (>609.8 sha): thermonuclear charge (deflection)

KEY FACT

Over the last 3,000 y, the planetary defense system has intercepted 11 potentially hazardous objects (3 of which were >0.4 mo). Thermonuclear deflection technology was used once (+2,110 y.c.) against an asteroid 0.4 mo in diameter.

9.8 Military Technology

9.8.1 General Context

KEY FACT

Xha'Ruun have not waged major wars for 2,780 y (since the Unity Treaty, −750 y.c.). Armed forces exist exclusively for defense and maintaining order. Military technology is not a top priority.

Component Strength Function

9.8.2 Limitations

  • Biological weapons — prohibited by international law (Memory of the Red Rot)
  • Weapons of mass destruction — limited (a few thermonuclear devices are kept)
  • Space weapons — placement of weapons in orbit is prohibited (Unity Treaty)

9.10 Environmental Technology

9.10.1 Climate Management

Xha'Ruun do not manage the planet's climate on a global scale — this is recognized as too risky. However, local regulation is practiced:

  • Desert irrigation (4% of Kharak's area)
  • Reforestation (+12% forest cover over the last 500 y)
  • Ocean protection from overfishing

9.10.2 Recovery After the Era of Crisis

The Era of Crisis (−2,100 – −750) was caused in part by ecological collapse (deforestation, erosion). Modern technologies allow Xha'Ruun to maintain ecological balance:

  • Recycling: 94% of waste is processed
  • Energy: 100% renewable/fusion (zero carbon footprint)
  • Mining: predominantly asteroid-based (for metals), not terrestrial
