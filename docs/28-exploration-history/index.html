Chapter 28: History of Théxar Exploration

"First we looked at the mountains and thought they were eternal. Then we looked at the mountains and saw history in them. Now we look at the mountains and hear their voice — the voice of a planet telling us its biography." — Academician Vel-Khal, "History of Planetary Exploration," +3 900 E.U.

Related sections: → see [Ch.07, "History of Civilization"], → see [Ch.09, "Technologies"], → see [Ch.30, "Planet in Numbers"] Volume: 30 pages Key principle: systematic exploration of Théxar went through 4 stages: geographical (before −750), scientific (−750 – +480), orbital (+480 – +2 000), and digital (+2 000 – present).

28.1 Era of Geographical Discoveries

Before the unification of continents (−750 E.U.), each continent developed cartography independently.

28.1.1 Kharak — Cradle of Cartography

Period Event Significance
~−9 000 First maps of Kharak (clay tablets) Rivers, mountains, settlements in the Thar valley
~−5 500 Expedition "Around Kharak" Proof that the continent is an island
~−2 000 Creation of the "Thar Map" First detailed map of the river (1:100 000)
~−1 500 Discovery of the Ven-Khal Strait Sea route to Rhuval

28.1.2 Rhuval and Vexar — Isolated Development

Rhuval developed cartography along its coasts. Vexar, being isolated, had its own traditions — with verbal descriptions of coastlines (without numerical coordinates).

28.1.3 Unification of Knowledge

−750 E.U. — after the Unity Treaty, maps of the three continents were compiled into a single atlas "Vokh-The-Ven" (World-Planet-Truth). Error in distance determination — no more than 5%.

28.2.1 First Orbital Flight

+480 E.U. — the spacecraft "The-Ven" (Planet-Truth) launched from the Khar-Vexar cosmodrome. Pilot — Shar-Ven. The flight lasted 2.4 hours, orbital altitude — 48.8 mo. Achievements:

  • First photographs of the planet from orbit
  • Confirmation of spherical shape
  • Measurement of exact radius
