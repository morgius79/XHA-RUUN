Volume IV: Language — Chapter 4: 14 Cases

"14 cases — 14 questions that can be asked of an object. From where? To where? Where? With what? With whom? About what? The Xha'Ruun language does not allow ignorance to persist." — Grammar Reference, Khar-Vexar Academy

Related sections: → see [Volume IV, Ch.3, "Grammar"] Length: 25 pages

4.1 Case System

No. Case Suffix Question Example
1 Nominative -∅ Who? What? xha'ruun — people
2 Ergative -sha Who? (agent) xha'ruun-sha — people (do)
3 Accusative -thar Whom? What? xha'ruun-thar — people (see)
4 Dative -ven To whom? xha'ruun-ven — to the people
5 Genitive -mor Whose? xha'ruun-mor — of the people
6 Instrumental -khal With what? xha'ruun-khal — by/with the people
7 Comitative -rass With whom? xha'ruun-rass — together with the people
8 Ablative -tor From where? xha'ruun-tor — from the people
9 Inessive -the Where? (inside) xha'ruun-the — in the people
10 Superessive -vex Where? (on) xha'ruun-vex — on the people
11 Allative -khar To where? xha'ruun-khar — toward the people
12 Translative -el Become what? xha'ruun-el — (turn into) the people
13 Essive -en Be what? xha'ruun-en — (as) the people
14 Terminative -mor-thal Until when? xha'ruun-mor-thal — up to the people

End of Chapter 4, Volume IV.
