Volume IV: Language — Chapter 2: Phonology

"The throat of a Xha'Ruun is a musical instrument. Within it, 24 consonants and 8 vowels — 32 notes from which an infinite symphony of speech is composed." — Academic of Phonetics, Khal-Ven

Related sections: → see [Volume IV, Ch.1, "Phonetics"] Length: 20 pages

2.1 Phonotactics

Syllable structure: (C)(C)V(C)(C)

Position Possible Sounds
C₁ (initial) Any consonant
C₂ (initial) r, l, w, j
V (nucleus) Any vowel
C₃ (final) Any consonant
C₄ (final) s, ʃ, t, d, n

2.2 Stress

Dynamic, on the first syllable of the root. Non-phonemic (does not change word meaning).

2.3 Vowel Harmony

Assimilation by backness: suffixes adjust to the vowel of the root.
