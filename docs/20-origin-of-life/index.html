Chapter 20: Origin of Life on Théxar

"In the beginning was not Light. In the beginning was Matter that learned to remember itself. We are not a creation. We are a continuation. Memory become flesh." — From the "Book of Origin," canonical text of Xha'Ruun, Era of Unity

Related sections: → see [Ch.04, "Biochemistry"], → see [Ch.12, "Geological Evolution"], → see [Ch.05, "Species Xha'Ruun"] Volume: 35 pages Key principle: life on Théxar arose ~4.2 billion cycles ago in H₂S-rich hydrothermal vents of the Gharra ocean. The key difference — a 6-letter genetic code, which emerged as an adaptation to elevated cosmic radiation.

Introduction

The origin of life on Théxar is one of the fundamental chapters of the encyclopedia. It connects the planet's geological history (Ch.12) with biochemistry (Ch.04) and the evolution of species (Ch.05, Ch.18).

On Théxar, abiogenesis took ~800 million cycles. The reason is a more complex chemical environment, in which the formation of stable organic molecules required additional steps, but led to a more stable genetic code.

20.1.2 Site of Abiogenesis

The main "reactor" of prebiotic chemistry became the hydrothermal vents of the Gharra ocean, where three key conditions were met:

  1. Temperature gradient — from 235.4°X (vent) to 47.3°X (surrounding water)
  2. pH gradient — from acidic (vent, pH 4–5) to slightly acidic (ocean, pH 6.5)
  3. Catalytic surfaces — pyrite (FeS₂), clay, silicon sediments
  4. Constant energy supply — chemical (H₂S, H₂, CH₄) and thermal

20.2 Prebiotic Chemistry

20.2.1 Abiogenic Synthesis of Organics

Experimental data (reproduced by Xha'Ruun civilization in laboratory conditions) show the following path of abiogenesis:

Stage 1: Formation of "soups" (4.2–4.0 billion cycles ago) In the atmosphere of early Théxar (CO₂+N₂+H₂S) under the action of UV radiation and lightning discharges, the following were synthesized:

  • Formaldehyde (CH₂O)
  • Hydrogen cyanide (HCN)
  • Hydrogen sulfide (H₂S)
  • Thioacetate (CH₃COSH — key for sulfur metabolism)

Stage 2: Concentration (4.0–3.8 billion cycles ago) Hydrothermal cycles and evaporation in tidal zones concentrated organics in porous silicon matrices.

Stage 3: First polymers (3.8–3.6 billion cycles ago) On the surface of pyrite (FeS₂) and clay minerals, polymerization occurred:

  • Amino acids → peptides
  • Nucleotides → RNA
  • Silicic acids → polysiloxanes

20.2.2 The 6-Letter Code Hypothesis

The emergence of the 6-letter genetic code is the key puzzle of Théxar's abiogenesis. The dominant theory is the radiation protection hypothesis:

  1. High UV flux of early Théxar caused frequent mutations in short RNA-like chains
  2. Additional bases (P — pyrimidine-N, X — xanthosine) increased chain stability and provided coding redundancy
  3. The six-letter code gave 6³ = 216 codons (versus 4³ = 64 for a four-letter code), which allowed:
    • Encoding 24 amino acids (versus 20)
    • Having ~9× redundancy (versus 3×)
    • 5× higher mutation resistance

KEY FACT

The six-letter code is not an accident of evolution, but a direct response to radiation pressure. Laboratory experiments by Xha'Ruun showed that under UV irradiation, 4-letter RNAs degrade 5× faster than 6-letter ones. At the radiation background of early Théxar (3.5× higher than modern), 6-letter systems had a decisive advantage.

20.3 From Chemistry to Life

20.3.1 Protocells (3.6–3.4 billion cycles ago)

The first protocells formed in the hydrothermal vents of Gharra. Their key features:

  • Membrane: silicon-carbon lipids (Si-C), more stable at high T than phospholipids
  • Metabolism: chemoautotrophic (Fe-S → FeS₂ + H₂ → energy)
  • Genetics: RNA world with 6-letter code
  • Reproduction: division (without sexual process)

20.3.2 First Cells (3.4–3.2 billion cycles ago)

The emergence of the first true cells (prokaryotes) marked the beginning of biogeochemical cycles:

3.5 billion cycles ago First chemoautotrophs Fe-S → FeS₂ + energy
3.4 billion cycles ago Fermentation First heterotrophic metabolism
3.2 billion cycles ago First phototrophs Use of IR spectrum (Khar'Vex)
3.0 billion cycles ago Emergence of chromophyll Efficient photosynthesis

20.3.3 Oxygen Catastrophe (4.0–3.8 billion cycles ago)

KEY FACT

The oxygen catastrophe on Théxar occurred 1.6 billion cycles earlier (4.0 vs. 2.4 billion cycles ago), due to the earlier emergence of efficient photosynthesis (chromophyll). However, it was more gradual — O₂ accumulated in the atmosphere not over 200 million cycles, but over ~600 million cycles, allowing anaerobic forms to adapt.

Consequences:

  1. Mass extinction of anaerobic forms (survived only in deep-water and underground refugia)
  2. Oxidation of dissolved Fe²⁺ in the oceans → formation of banded iron formations (BIF)
  3. Formation of the ozone layer
  4. Evolution of aerobic metabolism

Chapter Summary

Key conclusions:

  1. Life on Théxar arose ~4.2 billion cycles ago in H₂S-rich hydrothermal vents of the Gharra ocean.
  2. The 6-letter genetic code — adaptation to elevated UV radiation of early Théxar.
  3. The oxygen catastrophe occurred 1.6 billion cycles earlier (4.0 billion cycles ago).
  4. The dominant class — Thermochromadae, which appeared ~350 million cycles ago.
  5. First sapient beings (Xha'ri) — ~15,000 years ago.
  6. The six-letter code provides 5× higher genome error tolerance.
  7. Cambrian radiation on Théxar occurred ~350 million cycles ago.
