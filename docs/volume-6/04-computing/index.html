Volume VI: Technology — Chapter 4: Living Computation

"A processor is not silicon. A processor is a colony. 10¹² molecules, each one a neuron. We do not program — we teach." — Architect of the first bio-colony, +1,800 Y.E.

Related sections: → see [Volume VI, Ch.1], [Volume VI, Ch.3] Length: 30 pages

4.1 Bio-Optical Colonies

Xha'Ruun do not build processors in the terrestrial sense. They grow computing colonies — modified organisms consisting of billions of light-sensitive cells.

Principle: A colony is a film ~2 mm thick, composed of genetically modified cells. Each cell is simultaneously a photosensor, a processor, and a memory cell. The colony "thinks" optically: information is transmitted within it by photons, not electrons.

4.2 Planetary Processor

The largest computing colony — The-Ven ("Earth-Truth") — is located beneath the Thar valley. It is a biofilm ~240 km² in area, grown over 400 years.

Capabilities of The-Ven:

  • Climate modeling of the planet 100,000 years into the future
  • Evolution simulation of the biosphere (92% accuracy)
  • Calculation of gravitational resonances for GR-corridors
  • Real-time processing of all Xha-Ven network data

4.3 Crystal Memory (Ven-Khal)

A complement to living computation — long-term memory in silicon-carbide crystals (see Ch.3).

Ven-Khal crystals are not merely storage. They are used as associative "dictionaries" for bio-colonies. A colony does not "read" a crystal sequentially — it absorbs the crystal's information instantaneously in a single flash of light.

4.4 Limitations

  • Bio-colonies are slower than electronic circuits on simple operations (addition, comparison)
  • Cannot operate in a vacuum (require an atmosphere)
  • Sensitive to radiation (despite 5× Xha'Ruun resilience)
  • "Forget" information if not "fed" light of a specific spectrum
