Chapter 17: Flora of Théxar

"The earth of Théxar did not give birth to us from emptiness — she grew us from her forests, as a tree grows branches. We are her leaves, and when we depart, we return to her, as leaves return to the soil." — From the "Book of Roots," sacred text of Xha'Ruun, Era of Unity

Related sections: → see [Ch.11, "Ecology"], → see [Ch.13, "Continents"], → see [Ch.04, "Biochemistry"] Volume: 35 pages Key principle: Théxar's flora is based on chromophyll — a photosynthetic pigment with an absorption peak of 570 nm, adapted to the Khar'Vex spectrum (K2V).

Introduction

The plant world of Théxar is the foundation upon which the entire biosphere of the planet rests. From microscopic ocean phytoplankton to the vast forests of Vexar — Théxar's plants form the primary level of the trophic pyramid, converting Khar'Vex energy into biomass.

The fundamental difference of Théxar's flora is its adaptation to the orange spectrum of the star Khar'Vex (K2V, emission peak ~570 nm). Théxar's plants use chromophyll — a family of pigments with an absorption peak in the yellow-orange region (550–590 nm). It is chromophyll that gives Théxar's vegetation its characteristic hue — from copper-red to golden-bronze.

In this chapter, we examine in detail the plant kingdom of Théxar: its evolution, systematics, key species by biome, agricultural crops, and interaction with Xha'Ruun civilization.

17.1 Biochemistry of Photosynthesis

KEY FACT

Photosynthesis on Théxar is based on the pigment chromophyll (C₅₅H₇₀MgN₄O₆) with an absorption peak of 570 nm. Photosynthesis efficiency — 4.2%.

17.1.1 Chromophyll

Parameter Chromophyll (Théxar)
Absorption peak 570 nm (yellow-orange)
Plant color Copper-red, golden
Quantum efficiency 0.92
Photosynthesis efficiency 4.2%
Temperature optimum +60.3°X
Light saturation 420 W/m²

Chromophyll is not the only photosynthetic pigment. Additional pigments (carotenoids, phycobilins) extend the absorption spectrum, capturing light in the blue region (420–480 nm) and transferring energy to chromophyll.

17.1.2 Additional Pigments

Pigment Color Absorption Peak Role
Chromophyll a Copper-red 570 nm Primary, photosystem I
Chromophyll b Golden 540 nm Accessory, photosystem II
Theta-carotene Orange 480 nm Photoprotection
Xanthophyll Ther Yellow 450 nm Heat dissipation
Phycobilin Thal Purple 620 nm Deep-water (algae)

17.1.3 Concentrating Metabolism

Many Théxar plants use a mechanism for concentrating CO₂ in bundle sheath cells, which allows:

  • Reducing photorespiration (40% less loss)
  • Working efficiently at low CO₂ concentration while maintaining water balance
  • Saving water (25% lower transpiration)

Over 60% of Théxar plant species use concentrating metabolism.

17.2 Systematics of Flora

17.2.1 Overview of the Plant Kingdom

Théxar's flora numbers ~380,000 described plant species, of which ~320,000 are vascular (terrestrial) and ~60,000 are algae and mosses.

17.2.2 Chromophyta — Dominant Division

Chromophyta ("red-leaved") are the dominant group of terrestrial plants, occupying a wide range of ecological niches. Characterized by:

  • Presence of chromophyll a/b
  • Developed vascular system (vessels with silicon-reinforced walls)
  • Flowers adapted to pollination by local insects and wind
  • Fruits with high protein and lipid content

17.2.3 Silophyta — Silicon Plants

A unique division — plants that deposit amorphous silica (SiO₂) in their cell walls.

PROFILE: Silophyta

Parameter Value
SiO₂ content in dry mass 8–35%
Tissue hardness (Mohs) 3.5–5.5
Height 0.12–4.9 ша
Distribution Arid zones (Shan, highlands of Rhuval)
Adaptation Protection from herbivores + radiation

Silicon plants are dominants in arid and highland regions, where tissue hardness protects them from herbivores and the silicon layer protects against excessive UV radiation.

17.3 Key Species by Biome

17.3.1 Equatorial Jungles (south Kharak, north Rhuval)

Species Division Height Diameter Feature

Xha-Ven (Wise Fern) Thexophyta 2.4–4.9 ша — Epiphyte, filters H₂S from air

Tor-Khar — a giant tree. Reaches a height of 104 ша, trunk diameter up to 6.1 ша. Crown is at 73–104 ша, beneath it a special microclimate forms: humidity ~95%, temperature 2.7–4.3°X lower than surrounding. A single Tor-Khar tree can support up to 200 epiphyte species.

Ven-Shar — the dominant species of Vexar's temperate forests. Its wood is valued in construction for hardness and resistance to biocorrosion.

17.4.1 Kharak

Kharak's vegetation is the most diverse on the planet due to its latitudinal gradient (5 climate zones).

17.4.2 Rhuval

Rhuval, elongated from north to south, has fewer zone types but a unique mountain flora.

Zone Dominants Endemism
Tropical forests (north) Tor-Khar (up to 73 ша), Thex-Rice (wild) 15%
Savannas (center) Ash-Grass, Sun-Thorn 12%
Highlands (Raal plateau) Silophyta (silicon), High-Moss 42%
Subpolar forests (south) Needle-Bark, Ice-Thorn 28%

The highland flora of the Raal plateau is a unique endemic complex, isolated at altitudes of 3,049–5,854 ша. 42% of species are found nowhere else on the planet.

17.4.3 Vexar

Vexar is the continent with the highest level of endemism (31% of species).

  • Forests of Vexar — dominated by Ven-Shar, but with a unique understory of Thexophyta (epiphytes)
  • Endemic silicon cacti — 25 species of Silophyta not found on other continents
  • Mangrove forests — on the southern coast, unique root system filtering salt through silicon membranes

17.5.2 Ecological Functions of Forests

  • Oxygen balance: Théxar's forests produce ~62% of atmospheric oxygen (oceanic phytoplankton — 35%, others — 3%)
  • Carbon cycle: Théxar's forests sequester ~27.3 billion ру of CO₂ per year
  • Water cycle: Forest transpiration provides ~55% of precipitation over continents
  • Temperature regulation: Forests of Vexar lower local temperature by 2.2–4.3°X

17.6.2 Medicinal Plants

Xha'Ruun pharmacopoeia includes ~1,200 plant species used in medicine:

  • Khal-Ven (life-truth) — broad-spectrum antibiotic from Ven-Shar bark
  • Mor-Thorn (time thorn) — immunomodulator that slows aging
  • Gharra-Kelp — source of anticoagulants and anti-inflammatory compounds

17.6.3 Domesticated Plants

The process of plant domestication began ~10,000 years ago on the Central Plain of Kharak. Main domesticated crops:

  • Thex-Rice — ~8,000 years of cultivation, over 200 varieties
  • Khar-Grain — ~7,000 years, 90 varieties
  • Tor-Fruit — ~5,000 years, 60 varieties
  • Khal-Nut — ~4,000 years, 30 varieties
