Chapter 33: Fusion and the Energy of Stars

"We ignited a star on this planet. Not the one in the sky — the one in the reactors. And we hold it in a cage of magnetic field." — First Chief Engineer of the Khar-Vexar Fusion Complex, +1 200 E.U.

Related sections: → see [Ch.09, "Technologies"], → see [Ch.19, "Resources"], → see [Ch.24, "Moons"] Volume: 25 pages Key principle: fusion power — the foundation of Xha'Ruun energy (62% of the balance). Two main cycles: thorium (Th → U-233, molten salt reactors) and He-3 (D + ³He, magnetic confinement).

33.1 Thorium Cycle

33.1.1 Principle

The thorium fuel cycle — a process in which ²³²Th (the only natural isotope of thorium) captures a neutron and transforms into ²³³U — fissile material:

²³²Th + n → ²³³Th (β⁻, 22.3 min) → ²³³Pa (β⁻, 27.0 days) → ²³³U (fission)

Advantage: burnup of ~98% of fuel (vs. ~1% in uranium reactors).

33.2 He-3 Cycle

33.2.1 Reaction

D + ³He → ⁴He (3.6 MeV) + p (14.7 MeV)

The D-³He reaction — the "holy grail" of fusion. Its advantages:

  • Products: ⁴He + proton (not neutrons) — minimal radioactivity
  • High energy yield: 18.3 MeV per reaction
  • No reactor material activation
