Chapter 3: Planet Thexar

"She is not the largest, not the richest, not the oldest. But she is the only one who gave birth to us. And there is no other like her in all the galaxy." — From "Hymn to Thexar," classical work of the Flourishing Era

Related sections: see [Ch.01, "Cosmology"], see [Ch.02, "Star System"], see [Ch.04, "Biochemistry of Life"] Volume: 55 pages

Introduction

Thexar (/ˈθɛk.sɑr/ — "earth-mother" in ancient Xha'Ruun) is the third planet of the Khar'Vex system and the only known body in the galaxy where life arose and developed. It is a world of astonishing geological diversity: from scorching equatorial deserts to polar ice caps; from the deepest ocean trenches to mountain peaks exceeding 9,500 sha.

From a planetological perspective, Thexar is distinguished by longer days, a more ancient and calm geological regime, a different atmospheric composition, and two small moons. These features shaped the unique evolutionary trajectory of local life — and, ultimately, intelligence.

3.1 Origin and Geological Evolution

3.1.1 Formation

Thexar formed ~6.5 billion cycles ago in the protoplanetary disk of Khar'Vex through accretion of planetesimals enriched with heavy elements (protoplanetary cloud metallicity: [Fe/H] = +0.12).

3.1.3 Plate Tectonics

Thexar has active plate tectonics — the movement of lithospheric plates across the asthenosphere surface. This process is a key factor in the carbon cycle and long-term climate stability.

TABLE 3.1: Lithospheric Plates of Thexar

Plate Type Area (million mo²) Direction Speed (sha/year)
Thalas (oceanic) Oceanic 1.98 NW 3.9
Kharrak (continental) Continental 1.74 NE 3.4
Ruval (continental) Continental 1.16 S 5.0
Gharra (oceanic) Oceanic 1.82 SE 3.0
Vexar (continental) Continental 0.91 W 2.3
Northern (oceanic) Oceanic 0.74 E 6.5
Marginal (6 small) Mixed 0.08–0.33 each Various 0.6–8.5

Plate boundary types:

  • Divergent (spreading): mid-ocean ridges — 4 main (Thalass, Southern, Western, Northern)
  • Convergent (colliding): subduction zones — 6 active (along the edges of continent Kharak and Rhuval)
  • Transform (sliding): 4 major faults (largest — Thash Fault, ~3,900 mo long)

KEY FACT

The average speed of tectonic movements on Thexar is 2.8 sha/year. This is due to the slow cooling of the mantle (lower mass but effective insulation). Slow tectonics means longer geological cycles — in particular, the carbon cycle takes ~120 million years.

The elevated CO₂ content (0.8%) is not a sign of a greenhouse catastrophe. Thexar is in a state of equilibrium greenhouse effect: the higher CO₂ concentration compensates for lower insolation (0.41 stellar luminosity at 0.63 AU). Cumulative effect: average temperature +51.6°X.

3.2.2 Sky Color

The color of Thexar's sky is one of its most striking visual features. Due to:

  1. Elevated CO₂ content (absorbs in the blue region)
  2. Presence of H₂S (scatters differently)
  3. Orange-yellow spectrum of Khar'Vex (peak ~570 nm)

— the sky of Thexar has a characteristic golden-bronze hue, transitioning to orange near the horizon and rose-purple closer to the zenith. Sunsets are deep orange with red flashes.

Sky Color Reason
Noon Golden-bronze Scattering + CO₂ + star spectrum
Sunset Deep red Long path through H₂S layer
Twilight Purple Atmospheric glow

3.2.3 Weather and Climate

Global atmospheric circulation is determined by:

  1. Slow planetary rotation (27.3 h)
  2. Active tectonics (CO₂ and SO₂ emissions)
  3. Ocean currents
  4. Axial tilt (14.2°)

Circulation cells:

Thexar has a 6-cell model of atmospheric circulation:

  • Kharrak cell (equatorial): 0°–28° — ascending motion, abundant precipitation
  • Tor cell (temperate): 28°–55° — descending motion, dry zones
  • Thela cell (polar): 55°–90° — weak, cold

Due to slow rotation, the cells are wider and weaker — trade winds and monsoons are less pronounced, but winds are generally more predictable.

3.4.1 Equatorial Zone

Hot and humid belt encompassing the central regions of Thexar. Characterized by:

  • Average annual temperature +63.6°X (daily range: 8–12°C → 4–7°X)
  • Two rainy seasons (after equinoxes)
  • Dense multi-tiered jungles with high biomass
  • Red soils (iron oxides — "ferralite")
  • Biodiversity: ~70% of all species on the planet

3.4.2 Polar Regions

Due to the axial tilt (14.2°), the polar regions of Thexar are covered by a permanent ice sheet.

3.6.2 Radiation Belt

Thexar has two radiation belts:

  • Inner belt: altitude 1,463–5,488 sha, consists of high-energy protons
  • Outer belt: altitude 9,756–21,951 sha, consists of electrons

KEY FACT

The magnetosphere is one of the reasons the Xha'Ruun developed increased radiation resistance (see [Ch.05, "Physiology"]). Evolution "got used" to a somewhat higher level of cosmic radiation penetrating through the weakened magnetosphere.

3.7.2 Unique Ecosystems

Deep-sea hydrothermal vents — in the Gharra Ocean, at depths of 4,268–9,146 sha, ecosystems based on H₂S chemosynthesis have formed around 6 hydrothermal vent fields (see [Ch.04, "Biochemistry"]). These "oases" of life contain endemic species found nowhere else on the planet.

Cave ecosystems — in the limestone massifs of Kharak and Vexar, large subterranean ecosystems have been discovered (total area ~9,917 mo²), isolated from the surface for millions of years. Many species are blind, depigmented.

RELATION

The biodiversity of Thexar and key evolutionary adaptations are described in detail in see [Ch.04, "Biochemistry"] and see [Ch.05, "Xha'Ruun Species"].

3.10 Thexar in Culture

QUOTE

"Three continents — three characters. Kharak — old, wealthy, wise. Rhuval — young, passionate, warlike. Vexar — detached, mysterious, proud. So say the geographers. But the inhabitants of each are certain that their continent is the best. And each is right in their own way."

— "Geography for Travelers," popular edition, +2,500 y.E.U.

In Xha'Ruun culture, Thexar is perceived as a living entity — the "Great Mother." The oceans are associated with the circulatory system, the mountains with the skeleton, the winds with breath. This metaphor is deeply rooted in the language: the verb "to breathe" (shal-ven) and "to blow (of wind)" (shal-thar) share a common root.

RELATION

The attitude toward the planet as a living being is not only a poetic metaphor but also the foundation of Xha'Ruun ecological ethics (see [Ch.08, "Culture"]), as well as an element of religious worldview (see [Ch.10, "Mythology"]).

Chapter 3 Summary

Key Takeaways

  1. Thexar is a geologically active planet with a fully differentiated core, plate tectonics (7 main plates), and volcanism
  2. Atmosphere is rich in CO₂ (0.8%) — this is compensation for low insolation, not a greenhouse catastrophe
  3. The sky is golden-orange due to atmospheric composition and stellar spectrum
  4. Hydrosphere: 2 oceans, 5 seas, 67% of surface covered by water
  5. 3 continents: Kharak (1.41 million mo²), Rhuval (0.90 million mo²), Vexar (0.76 million mo²)
  6. Magnetosphere is moderate (42 µT), atmosphere compensates for its weakening
  7. Rich mineral resources — especially Fe, Cu, Th, Pt on Kharak
  8. Energy: 62% — thermonuclear, solar and hydroelectric power
