Chapter 4: Biochemistry of Life

"Our life is written in six letters. Our proteins are a mirror reflection. Our membranes sing at a silicon frequency. And yet — we live. Is this not a miracle?" — Xenobiochemist Thash-Ven, "Comparative Biochemistry: Thexar and Hypothetical Worlds," Era of Unity

Related sections: see [Ch.03, "Planet Thexar"], see [Ch.05, "Xha'Ruun Species"] Volume: 45 pages

Introduction

Biochemistry is the language of life. All life on Thexar is built on a single biochemical standard — but this standard is unique.

Life on Thexar uses six nucleotides, 24 amino acids, D-chirality, and incorporates silicon into key structural components of the cell. The genetic code has 50% greater information capacity per nucleotide. Metabolic networks are more branched. And hydrogen sulfide (H₂S) is not a poison but a key metabolic signal.

This chapter is not just a description of alien biochemistry. It is the foundation for understanding how the Xha'Ruun are built, how their ecosystems function, and how they interact with the planet.

4.1 Molecular Basis

4.1.1 Solvent

Life on Thexar is based on water (H₂O). The intracellular fluid of all Thexar organisms contains ~3% ammonia (NH₃) as a cryoprotectant and solubility modifier.

TERM: Ammonia Modification

The presence of NH₃ in the cytoplasm:

  • Lowers the freezing point to −4.4°X (versus 45°X for pure water)
  • Increases the solubility of nonpolar compounds
  • Modifies pH buffer capacity (optimal cytoplasmic pH: 7.6–8.4)
  • Participates in nitrogen metabolism as a nitrogen donor
Parameter Thexar Cell
Primary solvent H₂O + 3% NH₃
pH 7.6–8.4
Freezing point −4.4°X
Ion content (mM) Na⁺ 15, K⁺ 140, Cl⁻ 12
Buffer system HCO₃⁻/CO₂ + NH₃/NH₄⁺

The redundancy of the six-letter code means:

  • High resistance to mutations (most substitutions do not change the amino acid)
  • Possibility of fine regulation of gene expression through codon frequency
  • Additional levels of epigenetic regulation

4.1.3 Amino Acids

Life on Thexar uses 24 proteinogenic amino acids, of which 20 are standard (in D-form) and 4 are unique:

Amino Acid Property Function
Selenomethionine (SeMet) Sulfur-containing Antioxidant, radioprotection
Tyrocyanine (Tcn) Aromatic + CN Electron transfer
Silicoasparagine (SiAsn) Silicon-based Structural, membrane channels
Hydroxyvaline (hVal) Polar Thermostability

TERM: D-Chirality

All amino acids of Thexar life are D-isomers. Sugars, conversely, are L-isomers. This means Thexar proteins cannot form functional hybrid structures with organisms of opposite chirality. This is a fundamental biochemical difference — the "chiral wall."

4.1.4 Cell Structure

Thexar cells are eukaryote-like, with a nucleus and organelles, but with a number of unique differences:

Silicon-carbon channels (Si-channels):

These are unique transmembrane protein structures containing silicon atoms in the active center. They perform the functions of:

  • Transport of ions and small molecules with high selectivity
  • Thermoregulation (expel excess heat)
  • Electrochemical signaling (silicon conductivity is higher than carbon)
  • Participation in biomineralization (SiO₂ deposition)

4.2.2 Photosynthesis

Due to the spectrum of Khar'Vex (see [Ch.02, "Khar'Vex"]), photosynthesis on Thexar is adapted to orange-red light (peak ~570 nm).

Unique Photosystem III — an additional protein complex. It absorbs in the UV-A region (320–380 nm) and uses this energy for additional XTP synthesis. The overall efficiency of photosynthesis on Thexar is high — an evolutionary adaptation to lower insolation.

4.2.3 Hydrogen Sulfide Metabolism

KEY FACT

H₂S (hydrogen sulfide) is not a toxin for Thexar life. On the contrary, it is a key metabolic signal. The concentration of H₂S in Thexar's atmosphere (0.0008%) is maintained biogenically — it is a product of anaerobic bacterial activity and a "byproduct" of photosynthesis.

Biological functions of H₂S:

  1. Substrate for chemosynthesis (in deep-sea vent and cave ecosystems)
  2. Signaling molecule (regulation of metabolism, stress response)
  3. Antioxidant (neutralization of free radicals)
  4. Substrate for synthesis of sulfur-containing amino acids (selenomethionine)
Organism Effect of H₂S (1 µM) Reason
Xha'Ruun Normal metabolism Adapted respiratory chain
Thexar plants Growth +15% Chemoautotrophic supplementation

4.2.4 Nitrogen Metabolism

Nitrogen on Thexar is fixed via an alternative pathway using ammonia (NH₃) from the intracellular environment. The key enzyme — aminase-6 — contains molybdenum and vanadium in its active center in a 1:2 ratio.

Process End product Energy cost (XTP)
Fixation N₂ → NH₃ Ammonia 12 XTP
NH₃ → amino acids Glutamate, glutamine 2 XTP
NH₃ → bases G, C, A, T, P, X Variable

4.3 Genetics and Heredity

4.3.1 Replication and Repair

Thexar DNA replicates with high precision. This is achieved through:

  1. Additional proofreading mechanisms (eccentric nuclease-3)
  2. Stabilization of the double helix by triple bonds P≡X
  3. Increased activity of repair systems (3× higher than standard)

4.3.2 Radiation Resistance

Due to Thexar's weak magnetosphere (see [Ch.03, "Magnetosphere"]) and Khar'Vex's more active stellar wind in the past, all life on Thexar evolved with increased radiation resistance:

Parameter Value
LD₅₀ (lethal dose, 50%) ~12 Gy
DNA repair after 1 Gy ~25 min
Antioxidant system activity 3×
Tolerance to UV-C (254 nm) 480 J/mo²

4.4 Origin of Life

4.4.1 Abiogenesis

Life on Thexar arose ~4.0 billion cycles ago in the tidal zones of the primordial ocean, rich in sulfur and phosphorus compounds. The key prerequisites were:

  1. High H₂S content in volcanic emissions
  2. Elevated silicon concentration in the primordial ocean
  3. Stable conditions in the center of the habitable zone (see [Ch.02, "Habitable Zone"])
  4. Enriched protoplanetary disk (metallicity +0.12)

4.4.2 RNA World and Code Evolution

Early evolution on Thexar passed through an "RNA world" — an epoch when catalytic RNA (ribozymes) served simultaneously as information carrier and catalyst. The six-letter code probably became fixed due to a selective advantage: triple bonds P≡X are more resistant to UV damage than double bonds A=T.

  6. Three photosystems instead of two — adaptation to the orange spectrum of Khar'Vex
