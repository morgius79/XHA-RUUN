Chapter 23: Cryosphere of Théxar

"Ice is the memory of water. In it, moments of ancient epochs are frozen, the breath of ancient winds. When ice melts, it tells its stories." — From the treatise "Word on Water," +3,400 Y.E.

Related sections: → see [Ch.14, "Oceans"], → see [Ch.22, "Hydrology"], → see [Ch.15, "Atmosphere"] Volume: 25 pages Key principle: Théxar's cryosphere — 339 thous. мо² of ice cover (~8% of surface), containing ~202 thous. мо³ of ice. Two polar ice sheets, mountain glaciers, subglacial lakes, and seasonal sea ice.

Introduction

Théxar's cryosphere is one of the planet's most important climate-forming systems. Ice sheets reflect up to 80% of stellar radiation back into space (ice albedo 0.6–0.8 vs. 0.1–0.2 for water), regulating the global heat balance. They also store the planet's climate history in the form of isotopic profiles of ice cores.

23.1 Polar Ice Sheets

Théxar's two polar ice sheets — the Northern (on the Kharak continent) and the Southern (on the polar continent covering the south pole) — contain ~96% of all the planet's ice.

The Northern sheet lies on the land of the Kharak continent — its base is above sea level (on average 488–976 ша). The Southern lies mostly on land, but its edges extend into the Gharra ocean, forming ice shelves covering up to 24,800 мо².

Total volume of mountain glaciers: ~1.51 мо³ (less than 1% of the entire cryosphere). Despite their small share, they play a critical role in water supply for arid regions — glacial feeding of the Khar-Tor and Thash-Ven rivers (45% of their flow) provides water for ~60% of the continent's population.

23.3.1 Ice Regime

The sea ice melt season begins in month Khar-Ven and reaches its minimum in month Tor-Mor. Freezing begins in month Khal-Ven. Fast ice thickness off the Kharak coasts reaches 3.0 ша.

23.5 Role in Climate

23.5.1 Albedo Effect

Théxar's cryosphere reflects ~8% of all incoming stellar radiation back into space. Without the ice sheets, the planet's albedo would decrease from 0.31 to 0.28, leading to warming of ~0.8°X.

23.5.2 Fresh Water Reserves

The ice sheets contain ~202 thous. мо³ of fresh water — 68% of all the planet's fresh water. For comparison: groundwater — 70.5 thous. мо³, rivers and lakes — 1,510 мо³.

23.5.3 Paleoclimate Records

Ice cores from the southern sheet (deepest borehole — 4,390 ша) contain a continuous climate record spanning the last 500 thousand cycles, including changes in temperature, atmospheric composition, and volcanic activity.

23.6 Cryosphere Change

In the last 500 years, sea ice area has decreased by ~12%, polar ice sheet volume by 2%. This is linked to a natural exit from a mini-glacial phase, not anthropogenic impact — CO₂ emissions from Xha'Ruun civilization are negligible (fusion energy).

23.7 Economic Significance

  • Water supply: 45% of the largest rivers' flow comes from glacial meltwater
  • Ice tourism: ~2 million visitors/year (ice shelves)
  • Research stations: 28 year-round on the polar ice sheets
  • Glaciological exploration: searching for minerals under the ice
