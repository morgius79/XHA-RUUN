## Introduction

ThГ©xar вҖ” the third planet of the Khar'Vex system, formed ~6.5 billion cycles ago. Its geological history is a chronicle in ten stages, from planetesimal accretion to the modern tectonic regime. The planet has a fully differentiated core, an active mantle, and plate tectonics вҖ” these very processes became the foundation upon which life later arose.

From a planetological perspective, ThГ©xar has a number of key differences from typical rocky planets: longer days, an older and calmer geological regime, a different atmospheric composition, and two small satellites instead of one large one. These features shaped the unique evolutionary trajectory of local life.

Geological evolution is not just the history of rocks. It is the history of the atmosphere (tectonics regulate COвӮӮ), the oceans (seafloor relief determines currents), and ultimately вҖ” the very possibility of civilization's existence. Without plate tectonics, there would be no magnetic field protecting against cosmic radiation. Without volcanism, there would be no mineral-rich soils. Without slow continental drift, there would not be the diversity of ecosystems we observe today.

## 12.1 Planet Formation

> **KEY FACT**
>
> The formation process of ThГ©xar took about 500 million cycles and ended ~6.0 billion cycles ago. The protoplanetary cloud of the Khar'Vex system was characterized by elevated metallicity ([Fe/H] = +0.12), which provided the planet's core with a rich supply of heavy elements.

### 12.1.1 Accretion and Growth

The formation of ThГ©xar began with the accretion of planetesimals вҖ” small solid bodies enriched in iron and nickel. As the protoplanet grew, gravitational compression and radioactive decay of short-lived isotopes (ВІвҒ¶Al, вҒ¶вҒ°Fe) heated the interior to the melting temperature of iron (~875В°X). Gravitational differentiation began: molten iron rushed toward the center, forming the core, while lighter silicate rocks rose to the surface.

Over the following 70 million cycles, the planet experienced intense bombardment вҖ” remnants of the protoplanetary disk impacted the surface, increasing mass and releasing thermal energy. By the end of this period, ThГ©xar had reached ~95% of its current mass.

### 12.1.3 Origin of Water

Water on ThГ©xar has a mixed origin:

- **~60%** вҖ” mantle degassing (volcanic emissions of water vapor)
- **~30%** вҖ” delivered by icy planetesimals from the outer system
- **~10%** вҖ” cometary material

The elevated metallicity of the protoplanetary cloud provided a greater amount of radioactive elements in the crust, which slowed the planet's cooling and prolonged the period of volcanic degassing. As a result вҖ” ThГ©xar received a significant volume of "internal" water, compensating for its somewhat smaller size.

### 12.2.1 Crust

The crustal thickness of ThГ©xar varies from 2.4 РјРҫ (under oceans) to 9.1 РјРҫ (under continents). Average thickness вҖ” 7.1 РјРҫ. The crust is divided into two types:

- **Oceanic crust** (~2.4вҖ“3.7 РјРҫ): basaltic composition, density ~2.8 РәС…/dLВі. Formed at rift zones, recycled in subduction zones. Average age вҖ” 180 million cycles.
- **Continental crust** (~6.1вҖ“9.1 РјРҫ): granite-metamorphic composition, density ~2.6 РәС…/dLВі. Older вҖ” continental cores date to 5.5вҖ“5.0 billion cycles.

> **KEY FACT**
>
> ThГ©xar's continental crust contains an anomalously high concentration of thorium (Th) вҖ” ~2.5Г— above standard. This is a result of the elevated metallicity of the protoplanetary cloud. This fact has colossal significance for the energy sector of Xha'Ruun civilization. вҶ’ see [Ch.09, "Energy"]

### 12.2.2 Mantle

ThГ©xar's mantle is a silicate shell ~582 РјРҫ thick. Key feature: the temperature at the core-mantle boundary is ~109В°X lower due to the planet's smaller size and correspondingly lower pressure at the center. This leads to:

- Lower convection speed
- Larger convective cells
- Longer crustal renewal cycle

The upper part of the mantle (asthenosphere) is a zone of partial melting where magma chambers originate. This is where magma feeding ThГ©xar's volcanoes is formed.

### 12.2.3 Core

ThГ©xar's core makes up ~32% of the planet's mass. It is divided into a liquid outer core (Fe-Ni-S) and a solid inner core (Fe-Ni-Si). Convection in the liquid outer core generates the planet's magnetic field.

### 12.4.2 Oxygen Catastrophe

> **KEY FACT**
>
> The oxygen catastrophe on ThГ©xar occurred 4.0вҖ“3.8 billion cycles ago. The process was gradual вҖ” the slower evolution of photosynthetic organisms under conditions of reduced insolation stretched it over a long period.

**Consequences of the oxygen catastrophe:**
1. Mass extinction of anaerobic life forms
2. Oxidation of dissolved iron in the oceans вҖ” formation of banded iron formations (BIF)
3. Formation of the ozone layer вҖ” protection from UV radiation
4. Change in atmospheric chemical composition: OвӮӮ from trace amounts to ~12%
