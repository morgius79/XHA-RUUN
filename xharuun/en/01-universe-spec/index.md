## Introduction

Any civilization is defined not only by its history or culture, but also by the universe in which it arose. The laws of physics are the first and most unyielding framework shaping the destiny of a sentient species. They determine whether interstellar travel is possible, how far one can penetrate the structure of matter, what energy sources are available — and, ultimately, what the ceiling of development is.

The Xha'Ruun universe (internal name: **Vokh-Rass** — "The Great Harmony") possesses unique characteristics that shaped the path of the civilization.

This chapter is the most fundamental layer of the encyclopedia. Everything that follows — stars, planets, life, civilization — will rest upon the physical reality established here.

## 1.1 Cosmology: Birth and Structure of the Universe

> **KEY FACT**
>
> The Xha'Ruun universe emerged 13.82 billion cycles ago in the Big Bang event. A minor difference in the strong interaction constant resulted in the large-scale structure of space — the distribution of galaxies, the amount of dark matter — differing noticeably from the standard model.

### 1.1.1 Singularity and Inflation

The Vokh-Rass universe emerged from a singular state — a point of infinite density and temperature — approximately 13.82 billion cycles ago. The first ~9.2 × 10⁻³⁴ mig after the birth of the universe, it passed through a phase of exponential expansion (inflation), increasing in size by a factor of ~10²⁶.

| Parameter | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Age | 13.82 billion cycles |
| Start of inflation | ~9.2 × 10⁻³⁷ mig |
| Duration of inflation | ~9.2 × 10⁻³⁴ mig |
| Temperature at Planck epoch | ~5.44 × 10³¹ °X |
| Baryogenesis (emergence of matter) | ~9.2 × 10⁻¹² mig |

### 1.1.2 Primordial Nucleosynthesis

In the first ~16 mig after the birth of the universe, when the temperature dropped to ~5.44 × 10⁸ °X, protons and neutrons began combining into nuclei of light elements.

**Key characteristic:** due to the constant αₛ = 0.1197, deuterium (²H) turned out to be slightly more stable, leading to the following distribution of primordial elements:

| Element | Mass fraction |
|---------|--------------|
| Hydrogen (¹H) | 73% |
| Helium-4 (⁴He) | 24.5% |
| Deuterium (²H) | 0.008% |
| Lithium-7 (⁷Li) | 0.00006% |
| Traces (³He, ⁶Li, ⁹Be) | 0.0008% |

> **IMPORTANT**
>
> The elevated deuterium content is one of the key cosmological characteristics. Deuterium plays a key role in thermonuclear reactions, and its excess will become one of the factors in the technological development of the Xha'Ruun civilization. see [Ch.09, "Energy"]

### 1.1.3 Recombination and Dark Ages

380,000 years after the Big Bang, the universe cooled enough for protons and electrons to combine into neutral atoms — the epoch of recombination. The universe became transparent to radiation, and we observe this "imprint" as the cosmic microwave background.

> **PROFILE: Vokh-Rass Cosmic Microwave Background**
>
> | Parameter | Value |
> |-----------|-------|
> | Average temperature | −102 °X |
> | Anisotropy | ~10⁻⁵ °X |
> | Spectrum | Black body |
> | Epoch of emission | ~380,000 years |

After recombination came the **"Dark Ages"** — an epoch when the universe was filled with neutral gas and no stars had yet ignited. This period lasted about 200 million years.

### 1.1.4 Formation of Large-Scale Structure

> **TERM: Large-Scale Structure**
>
> The distribution of galaxies and their clusters on scales from 100 million to 1 billion light-years. Characterized by the "cosmic web" — a network of filament structures of dark matter and gas, at the intersections of which galaxies form.

Thanks to the constant Ωᵈᵐ = 0.262, the formation of large-scale structure has the following characteristics:

- **Galaxies began forming ~50 million years earlier** than in standard chronology
- **The number of dwarf satellite galaxies is ~12% higher**
- **Galaxy clusters are on average 3–5% more massive**
- **Voids are larger** — dark matter "pulled" matter together more efficiently

## 1.2 Fundamental Forces and Particles

In Vokh-Rass, four fundamental interactions operate. Three are identical to the standard model. One — the strong nuclear interaction — exhibits a measurable difference.

### 1.2.1 The Four Forces

> **TABLE 1.1: Fundamental Interactions of Vokh-Rass**
>
> | Interaction | Relative Strength | Range | Carrier | Constant |
> |------------|-----------------|-------|---------|----------|
> | Strong Nuclear | 1 | ~1.2 × 10⁻¹⁵ sha | Gluons | **αₛ = 0.1197** |
> | Electromagnetic | 10⁻² | ∞ | Photons | α = 1/137 |
> | Weak Nuclear | 10⁻⁶ | ~1.2 × 10⁻¹⁸ sha | W⁺/W⁻/Z⁰ bosons | Standard |
> | Gravity | 10⁻³⁸ | ∞ | Gravitons (hypothetical) | G |

**Strong interaction** — the only force whose constant value differs from the standard model. The value αₛ = 0.1197 is sufficient to:

1. **Stabilize a number of unstable isotopes** (in particular, ¹⁰Be, ⁸B, and some transuranic elements)
2. **Increase the neutron capture cross-section** for deuterium and lithium nuclei
3. **Create an "island of stability"** in the region of superheavy elements (Z = 126–130) — elements stable for thousands of years.

> **RELATION**
>
> The existence of an island of stability for superheavy elements has a direct impact on the technological development of the civilization see [Ch.09, "Materials Science"]. Some of these elements are key components of alloys for the space elevator and thermonuclear reactors.

### 1.2.2 Standard Model (Baryonic Matter)

The Standard Model of particle physics in Vokh-Rass is supplemented by the existence of stable superheavy nuclei (Z = 126–130), forming a separate group — **transactinoids**.

| Group | Composition | Examples | Stability |
|-------|------------|----------|-----------|
| Quarks | u, d, s, c, b, t | proton (uud), neutron (udd) | Stable |
| Leptons | e, μ, τ, νₑ, νμ, ντ | electron, neutrino | Stable |
| Gauge bosons | γ, g, W⁺/W⁻, Z⁰ | photon, gluon | Stable |
| Superheavy nuclei | Z = 126–130 | **Khar'nium (Kh, Z=127)**, **Vexanium (Vx, Z=128)** | **Unique** |

> **TERM: Khar'nium (Kh)**
>
> Superheavy element with atomic number 127 and atomic mass ~327. Named after the star Khar'Vex. Stable due to the increased strong interaction constant. Half-life of the key isotope ³²⁷Kh is ~12,400 years. Possesses unique magnetic properties. see [APP:physical-ref]

Superheavy elements exist in nature in trace amounts (their formation occurs in neutron stars during collisions), but the Xha'Ruun have learned to synthesize them on an industrial scale. see [Ch.09, "Materials"]

### 1.2.3 Neutrinos

> **KEY FACT**
>
> The rest mass of the electron neutrino in the Vokh-Rass universe is 0.12 eV/c².

Heavier neutrinos:
- Move ~10% slower compared to the standard model
- Contribute more to dark matter (~12% of the hot component)
- Create denser neutrino halos around galaxies
- Influence nucleosynthesis in supernovae (modify element ratios)

**Practical significance:** neutrino astronomy in the Xha'Ruun civilization is especially well-developed due to the higher density of neutrino fluxes and the greater mass of neutrinos, simplifying their detection.

## 1.3 Spacetime and Causality

> **QUOTE**
>
> "Space is the canvas. Time is the brush. And causality is the one rule the artist cannot break."
>
> — Theoretical physicist Rhaal-Tor, treatise "The Geometry of Reality," Renaissance Era

### 1.3.1 Spacetime Metric

The spacetime of Vokh-Rass is described by a Lorentzian metric with signature (−, +, +, +) — a pseudo-Riemannian manifold where timelike intervals are negative and spacelike intervals are positive.

> **PROFILE: Geometry of the Universe**
>
> | Parameter | Value |
> |-----------|-------|
> | Spatial dimensions | 3 |
> | Temporal dimensions | 1 |
> | Metric type | Pseudo-Riemannian, Lorentzian |
> | Signature | (−, +, +, +) |
> | Global curvature | Ω = 1.00 ± 0.01 (flat) |
> | Expansion state | Accelerated |
> | Ultimate fate | Heat death |

### 1.3.2 Speed of Light and Causality

**Speed of light in vacuum:** c = 3.99 × 10⁹ sha/mig (6.65 × 10⁵ mo/mig) — absolute limit on information transfer speed. This is a fundamental law, unshakable in the Xha'Ruun universe.

> **IMPORTANT**
>
> **No information, matter, or energy can travel faster than the speed of light.** FTL drives (warp drives), hyperspace, "subspace communication," and other similar concepts are **physically impossible** in the Xha'Ruun universe. This is a fundamental limitation that the civilization has accepted and learned to work with.

The absence of FTL technologies is not a question of engineering complexity, but a fundamental impossibility protected by the very structure of spacetime. Alcubierre warp metrics, theoretically discussed by physicists of the Renaissance Era, would require exotic matter with negative mass, the existence of which has not been confirmed in the Vokh-Rass universe.

### 1.3.3 Speed of Time

Time in the Vokh-Rass universe flows at a fundamental rate against which all changes are measured. Time dilation in strong gravitational fields and at near-light speeds obeys the same equations of special and general relativity.

**Practical consequence for civilization:** interstellar travel — if it ever takes place — will be subject to the same limitations: crewed spacecraft can only reach the nearest stars within the lifespan of several generations.

### 1.3.4 Quantum Mechanics

The quantum mechanics of the Xha'Ruun universe is described by the same mathematical principles: the uncertainty principle, the Schrodinger equation, quantum entanglement. The interpretation, however, differs: the dominant philosophical school is the **"Evolutionary Many-Worlds"** — the branching of the wave function is considered not as parallel universes, but as evolutionary "potentials" that collapse upon observation.

> **PROFILE: Interpretations of Quantum Mechanics**
>
> | Aspect | Standard | Evolutionary (Xha'Ruun) |
> |--------|---------|------------------------|
> | WF Collapse | Abrupt upon observation | Evolutionary, through interaction |
> | Multiple worlds | Contentious | Rejected |
> | Role of observer | Central | Interaction ≠ observation |
> | Mathematical apparatus | = | = (Hilbert spaces) |

Differences in interpretation do not affect the predictive power of quantum physics — the equations are the same, and the Xha'Ruun have developed quantum technologies (quantum computing, cryptography) to a high level.

## 1.5 Dark Matter and Dark Energy

### 1.5.1 Dark Matter

~26% of the mass-energy of Vokh-Rass is dark matter — an invisible substance that does not interact with electromagnetic radiation but manifests itself through gravitational influence.

**Characteristic feature of Vokh-Rass:** higher density of dark matter (Ωᵈᵐ = 0.262) and a larger fraction of the hot component (sterile neutrinos — ~12% of all dark matter).

> **TABLE 1.5: Dark Matter Components of Vokh-Rass**
>
> | Component | Type | Fraction | Behavior |
> |-----------|------|----------|----------|
> | Cold (CDM) | WIMP-like particles | ~88% | Non-collisional, non-relativistic |
> | Hot (HDM) | Sterile neutrinos | ~12% | Relativistic, influences structure formation |

**Observed manifestations of dark matter in the Xha'Ruun universe:**

1. **Galaxy rotation curves:** stars at the edges of galaxies move faster than visible mass predicts
2. **Gravitational lensing:** light from distant galaxies is distorted by the gravitational field of invisible clusters
3. **Dark matter halos:** each galaxy is embedded in an extensive dark matter halo extending 5–10 times farther than the visible disk
4. **Structure formation:** the anisotropy of the cosmic microwave background carries the imprint of initial dark matter density fluctuations

### 1.5.2 Dark Energy

The dark energy of the Vokh-Rass universe is a cosmological constant Λ with a magnitude corresponding to Ωᵟ = 0.689. It is responsible for the accelerated expansion of the universe. The nature of dark energy remains a mystery to the Xha'Ruun — it is one of the biggest open questions in their cosmology.

> **KEY FACT**
>
> The Xha'Ruun cannot use dark matter or dark energy for practical purposes. These substances remain the domain of pure theoretical physics. "Null-space" technologies using dark energy as a source are the subject of science fiction within the Xha'Ruun civilization itself.

## 1.6 Fundamental Limitations

This section — perhaps the most important for understanding the technological "ceiling" of the civilization. While many science fiction universes offer warp drives, teleportation, and time travel, the Xha'Ruun universe imposes strict limitations.

> **TABLE 1.6: Fundamental "Impossibilities"**
>
> | Technology | Reason for Impossibility | Status in Xha'Ruun Science |
> |------------|------------------------|---------------------------|
> | FTL travel | Causality principle, special relativity | Axiomatically impossible |
> | Time travel | Causality, closed timelike curves | Axiomatically impossible |
> | Matter teleportation | Quantum information theory (no-cloning theorem) | Impossible (quantum teleportation of *state* — yes, of mass — no) |
> | Anti-gravity | Conservation laws, exotic matter not found | Theoretically impossible |
> | Perpetual motion machine | Second law of thermodynamics | Impossible |
> | Creation/destruction of energy | First law of thermodynamics | Impossible |
> | Complete determinism | Quantum mechanics, uncertainty principle | Impossible |

### 1.6.1 What Is Possible

Despite all limitations, the possibilities remain grand:

1. **Nuclear fusion** — conversion of matter into energy through the fusion of light nuclei. A practically inexhaustible energy source.
2. **Quantum computing** — using quantum superposition and entanglement for computations inaccessible to classical computers.
3. **Orbital infrastructure** — space elevators, orbital stations, colonies within the star system.
4. **Probes to nearest stars** — automated probes on light sails (flight time: 50–200 years).
5. **Asteroid mining** — access to virtually unlimited mineral resources.

## 1.7 Cultural Cosmology: The Xha'Ruun View of the Universe

> **QUOTE**
>
> "We live in an ocean of dark matter, and we cannot even touch it. But we know it is here — just as a blind man knows that a wall exists before him. Knowledge does not require touch."
>
> — Cosmologist Ven-Thal, "Lectures on the Invisible," Era of Unity

### 1.7.1 Cultural Significance of Cosmology

For the Xha'Ruun civilization, cosmology is not just a science. It is deeply intertwined with religion (see [Ch.10, "Mythology and Religion"]). The concept of the "Great Symphony" — a mythological representation of the universe as a harmonic oscillation — finds direct correspondence in the physical picture of the world:

- **The Primordial Vibration** (religious concept) ↔ **Quantum fluctuations** (physical concept)
- **The Seven Voices** ↔ **Four fundamental forces + dark matter, dark energy, spacetime**
- **The Great Breath** (universe cycle) ↔ **Cyclic cosmology** (theoretical model)

This synthesis of science and faith does not create conflict — most Xha'Ruun perceive physics as "the language in which the gods speak to the sentient."

### 1.7.2 Open Cosmological Questions

Even after millennia of scientific development, cosmology of the Xha'Ruun faces questions to which there are no answers:

1. **What was before the Big Bang?** — The singular state cannot be described
2. **Why are the physical constants what they are?** — This question remains open
3. **What is dark energy?** — Its nature remains unknown
4. **Is there other intelligent life?** — No confirmed signal has been received
5. **Is the universe unique?** — The multiverse theory remains speculative

> **KEY FACT**
>
> The Xha'Ruun civilization **has not detected** any other intelligent life forms in its galaxy. All attempts to find extraterrestrial signals — from radio to neutrino — have yielded no results. Thexar remains the only known habitable world. This has left a deep imprint on the culture: a sense of "cosmic solitude" and the associated responsibility for the preservation of life.

## 1.8 The Future of the Universe

Current cosmological models of Vokh-Rass predict the **Big Freeze** — a scenario in which the expansion of the universe continues forever, temperature approaches absolute zero, and all matter eventually decays.

> **CHRONOLOGY: Future of the Vokh-Rass Universe**
>
> | Time (from present) | Event |
> |--------------------|-------|
> | +1 billion cycles | Luminosity of Khar'Vex increases by ~10% — Thexar becomes uninhabitable |
> | +5 billion cycles | Khar'Vex leaves the main sequence, becomes a red giant |
> | +20 billion cycles | All stars except the smallest (red dwarfs) decay |
> | +100 billion cycles | The last stars go out — the universe plunges into darkness |
> | +10¹⁷ cycles | Disintegration of planetary systems (gravitational instability) |
> | +10³⁰ cycles | Black hole decay (Hawking radiation) |
> | +10¹⁰⁰ cycles | Heat death — absolute zero, entropy is maximal |

> **IMPORTANT**
>
> The prospect of the heat death of the universe is not a reason for pessimism in Xha'Ruun culture. Cyclic cosmology, interwoven with religious beliefs (see [Ch.10, "Eschatology"]), suggests that the "Great Breath" — expansion and contraction — may give way to a new cycle. Even if physically the Big Crunch is unlikely, philosophically the Xha'Ruun believe that the universe will "exhale" and begin a new inhale.

## Chapter 1 Summary

### Key Takeaways

1. **The Xha'Ruun universe is almost identical to the standard model** — 13.82 billion cycles, the same four forces, the same general relativity. The differences are minimal: αₛ is 0.4% higher than standard, neutrinos are twice as heavy, deuterium is 3.2× higher than standard abundance.
2. **The speed of light is the absolute limit.** No FTL technologies, warp drives, or subspace communication. This is fundamental.
3. **Superheavy elements are stable.** Z = 126–130 exist, which opens the path to unique materials.
4. **Dark matter and dark energy exist** but are unmanageable. Their nature is an open scientific problem.
5. **The Xha'Ruun civilization is alone** — no other intelligent species has been detected.
6. **The future of the universe is heat death** in ~10¹⁰⁰ cycles, but the culture maintains a cyclic worldview.
