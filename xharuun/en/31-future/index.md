## 31.1 Geological Future

| Term | Event | Confidence | Consequences |
|------|---------|-------------|-------------|
| 10 000 cycles | Next Tor-Ven eruption (supervolcano) | High (avg. interval 8 000 cycles) | −1.6°X for 5 cycles |
| 28 000 cycles | Next magnetic reversal | High (avg. 350 000 cycles, already overdue) | Field weakening by 90% for 3 000–5 000 cycles |
| 50 000 cycles | Next glaciation | High | T drop of −2.7°X |
| 100 000 cycles | Kharak shift 2° north | Moderate | Climate change |
| 1 million cycles | New volcanic center in the Irkhen chain | High | Island formation |

### 31.1.1 Magnetic Reversal

The last reversal was ~28 000 cycles ago. The average interval is 350 000 cycles. The reversal is already "overdue" — possibly the current epoch (Brunhes-Théxar) is one of the longest in the planet's history.

## 31.3 Anthropogenic Influence

### 31.3.1 Minimal Impact

The Xha'Ruun civilization has minimal impact on the global ecosystem:

| Factor | Level |
|--------|---------|
| CO₂ emissions | <0.1% of natural flux |
| Land use | 5.8% of land (anthropogenic) |
| Logging | ~725 million sha³/year (0.5% of stock) |
| Waste | 95% recycling |

### 31.3.2 Forecasts

At the current level of impact, the civilization will not cause climatic changes comparable to natural cycles within the next 10 000 cycles.

Khar'Vex is an orange dwarf K2V, notable for its longevity. Théxar will remain in the habitable zone for at least 6–8 billion cycles.
