## Introduction

The total land area of Théxar is ~3.3 million mo² — about 33% of the planet's surface. Three continents — Kharak, Rhuval, and Vexar — differ in size, geological age, climate, and mineral resources. Each represents not just a geographical region but a separate world with its own formation history, ecosystem, and cultural tradition. Surrounding them are shelf seas, island archipelagos, and coral systems that complement the continental geography.

> **KEY FACT**
>
> All three continents of Théxar have different geological origins. Kharak is the oldest, its core formed ~5.5 billion cycles ago. Rhuval and Vexar are products of the breakup of the southern supercontinent Vaal-Kharak, completed ~1.0 billion cycles ago. → see [Ch.12, "Plate Tectonics"]

Kharak (from the root *khar-* — "light", "star") is the largest continent, the ancient core of the Xha'Ruun civilization. Its name reflects not only its geographical position (northern, under the light of Khar'Vex) but also its cultural role: it is here that the First Empire arose, here the capital is located, and here the majority of the Union's industry and science is concentrated.

### 13.2.1 Relief and Geology

Kharak is geologically the oldest — its craton (ancient core) has an age of ~5.5 billion cycles. The relief is characterized by three main zones:

- **Central Plain** — a vast lowland (0.34 million mo²) with elevations of 244–732 sha above sea level. Formed by ancient river deposits; today it is the planet's main agricultural breadbasket.
- **Khar-Tor Ridge** — a mountain range along the western coast, extending ~976 mo. Contains the highest peak of Théxar — Mount Verksan (9,537 sha). A zone of active orogenesis with rich ore deposits. → see [LOC-0052]
- **Shan Desert** — a rocky plateau in the southeast (0.14 million mo²). The surface consists of gravel plains and remnant ancient rocks. Rich in uranium and thorium.

### 13.2.2 Climate

Kharak spans five climate zones — from equatorial forests on the southern coast to polar deserts in the north:

| Zone | Share of continent | Average T (°X) | Precipitation (mm/year) |
|------|-------------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| Equatorial forests | 8% | 58.1…62.5 | 2,500–4,000 |
| Savannas | 18% | 54.9…60.3 | 800–1,500 |
| Temperate zone | 35% | 47.8…56.0 | 500–900 |
| Boreal (taiga) | 28% | 39.6…50.5 | 300–600 |
| Polar tundra | 11% | 31.5…47.8 | 100–300 |

### 13.2.3 Regions

- **Thar Delta** (0.03 million mo²) — southeastern coast where the Thar River flows into the Serrin Sea. The oldest cultural center; here lie the first settlements of civilization (→ see [EV-0001]) and the capital Khar-Vexar.
- **Serrin Plateau** (0.07 million mo²) — an ancient basalt plateau in the north, age >2 billion cycles. One of the oldest geological formations on the planet.
- **Northern Taiga** (0.17 million mo²) — an extensive zone of coniferous forests. An important source of biological resources.

### 13.2.4 Economic Significance

Kharak produces ~48% of the Xha'Ruun Union's GDP. Key industries: thorium and iron mining (Khar-Tor ridge), agriculture (Central Plain), high technology (Khar-Vexar), science and education. The continent houses 3 of the 12 guild headquarters.

Rhuval (from the root *rhuun* — "people", "community") is the second-largest continent, stretching from north to south. Due to its meridional orientation, it covers a vast range of climate zones: from tropical jungles in the north to glaciers in the south.

### 13.3.1 Relief and Geology

Rhuval formed as a result of the breakup of the supercontinent Vaal-Kharak ~2.5 billion cycles ago. Its geology is younger and more active:

- **Raal Plateau** — central highlands (1,463–3,415 sha above sea level), formed by mafic intrusions of the prototectonic era. Contains the richest deposits of platinum, chromium, and manganese. → see [LOC-0053]
- **Irkhen Mountain Chain** — a volcanic ridge along the northern coast. Highest point — Mt. Moraat (7,915 sha). Volcanic activity is above the planetary average.
- **Thash-Ven Valley** — a fertile alluvial plain along the continent's largest river.

### 13.3.2 Climate

|------|-------------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| Tropical forests | 15% | 57.0…61.4 | 3,000–5,000 |
| Savannas and woodlands | 22% | 54.9…59.2 | 1,000–2,000 |
| Subtropics | 25% | 51.6…57.0 | 500–1,200 |
| Temperate zone | 23% | 47.3…52.6 | 400–800 |

### 13.3.3 Economic Significance

Rhuval is the industrial heart of the Union: ~35% of GDP. Key industries: mining (Pt, Au, Ag, Cr), heavy industry, petrochemicals. The continent houses the largest industrial centers and ports.

Rhuval is also known for its military tradition — here are the main defensive structures and military academies.

> **IMPORTANT**
>
> Rhuval is the only continent with active volcanism that poses a threat to populated areas. The last major eruption (Irkhen chain, +3,890 Y.E.) led to the temporary evacuation of 2 million people. The volcano monitoring system on Rhuval is the most advanced on the planet.

Vexar (from the root *vex-* — "to give", "to create") is the smallest of the three continents, the most geographically and culturally isolated. Separated from Rhuval by the Ven-Khal Strait (width 85 mo at its narrowest point).

### 13.4.1 Isolation and Endemism

Vexar separated from Rhuval ~1.0 billion cycles ago and has since developed in isolation. This led to:

- **31% endemic plant species** — compared to 12% on Kharak and 18% on Rhuval
- A unique forest ecosystem dominated by silicon-enriched plant forms
- The absence of large terrestrial predators (the ecological niche is occupied by small pack species)

Vexar has no analogue among Théxar's continents in terms of endemism — 31% unique plant species with complete isolation on a continental scale.

### 13.4.2 Cultural Specificity

Vexar's isolation shaped a unique cultural tradition:

- The philosophical school of the "Third Way" (independence from Kharak's guilds)
- Its own architectural tradition (use of local black basalt)
- A developed system of nature reserves — 15% of the territory declared "Silence Zones" (→ see [Law of Silence], CANON.md §4.3)

### 13.4.3 Economic Significance

Vexar produces ~15% of GDP. Key industries: rare-earth elements (Ti, Mn, Cr, Co), biopharmaceuticals, quantum computing, tourism. The continent is known for its research institutes and biotechnology laboratories.

## 13.5 Island Territories

The total area of Théxar's islands is ~0.2 million mo² (~6% of land). Largest archipelagos:

**The Irkhen Archipelago** deserves special mention as a zone of cultural exchange and historical conflict between Kharak and Rhuval. Located in the Irkhen Sea between the two continents, the archipelago has served for millennia as a bridge — cultural, trade, and military. Each of the 24 inhabited islands of the archipelago has its own cultural tradition, synthesizing elements of both mainland civilizations.

Théxar's satellites — **Serrin** and **Thel** — are also formally territories of the Union, but belong not to the continents, but to extraplanetary holdings. → see [Ch.03, "Satellites"]

## 13.6 Ecological Zones

Despite the lesser diversity of latitudes (axial tilt 14.2°), Théxar displays a rich palette of ecosystems due to a combination of factors:

1. **Different elevations above sea level** — from coastal lowlands to mountains above 8,537 sha
2. **Rain shadow of mountain ranges** — the Shan Desert formed by the rain shadow of Khar-Tor
3. **Ocean currents** — the warm Khar-Vex current along Kharak's western coast, the cold Thel current along the eastern coast
4. **Geothermal oases** — on Rhuval's volcanic plateaus

## Chapter Summary

**Key takeaways:**
1. Théxar's land consists of three continents: Kharak (1.4 million mo²), Rhuval (0.9 million mo²), Vexar (0.8 million mo²) and islands (0.2 million mo²).
2. Kharak is the oldest continent, the core of Xha'Ruun civilization, ~39% of the planet's population.
3. Rhuval is the industrial center with high volcanic activity, ~32% of the population.
4. Vexar is an isolated continent with unique biota and culture, ~18% of the population.
5. Endemism on Vexar reaches 31% — 2.6× higher than on Kharak.
6. The Irkhen Archipelago is a zone of cultural synthesis between Kharak and Rhuval.
7. 10 major biomes, from tropical forests to polar deserts.
