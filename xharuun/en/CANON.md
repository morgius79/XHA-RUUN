## 1. Fundamental Constants of the Universe

The Xha'Ruun universe exists within the same four fundamental interactions as our known reality; however, some constants differ slightly, leading to a different large-scale structure of the cosmos and different chemistry of life.

### 1.1 Base Constants

| Constant | Symbol | Value | Rel. to Earth Universe | Note |
|----------|--------|-------|----------------------|------|
| Speed of light in vacuum | c | 2.998 × 10⁸ m/s | = | Identical |
| Gravitational constant | G | 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ m³·kg⁻¹·s⁻² | = | Identical |
| Planck constant | h | 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s | = | Identical |
| Fine-structure constant | α | 1/137.036 | = | Identical |
| Elementary charge | e | 1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ C | = | Identical |
| **Strong interaction constant** | **αₛ** | **0.1197** | **≠ (+0.0005)** | Slight difference — key factor for exotic isotope stability [⚠ see section 4] |
| **Neutrino rest mass (electron)** | **m(νₑ)** | **0.12 eV/c²** | **≠** | 2× heavier than Earth's — affects dark matter structure |

### 1.2 Spacetime

- **Spacetime dimensions:** 3+1 (three spatial + one temporal dimension)
- **Additional dimensions:** absent at macroscopic scales
- **Metric:** Lorentzian (pseudo-Riemannian), signature (−, +, +, +)
- **Global geometry:** spatially flat universe (Ω ≈ 1.00 ± 0.01)
- **Causality principle:** absolute; FTL information transfer is impossible [⚠ fundamental technology constraint — → see [Ch.09, "Technology"]]

| Dark energy density (Ωᵟ) | 0.689 | ← |
| Hubble constant (H₀) | 67.8 km/s/Mpc | ← |

### 2.2 Large-Scale Structure

- **Galaxy type:** barred spiral (SBbc), similar to the Milky Way
- **Galaxy diameter:** ~105,000 ly
- **Star system location:** in a spiral arm, ~26,000 ly from galactic center
- **Local group:** includes 3 large galaxies and ~40 dwarf galaxies
- **Supercluster:** Laniakea (analogous to Earth's Laniakea)

### 2.3 Dark Matter

- **Composition:** predominantly cold dark matter (WIMP-like particles), with a small (~12%) admixture of "hot" component (sterile neutrinos)
- **Distribution:** halo with excess density near the galactic disk (consequence of heavier neutrinos — → see [CANON, "Fundamental Constants"])

## 3. Star System

### 3.1 The Star

| Parameter | Value | Note |
|-----------|-------|------|
| Name | **Khar'Vex** ([T] /ˈxɑr.vɛks/) | "Light-Giver" in Old Xha'Ruun |
| Spectral class | **K2V** (orange dwarf) | [⚠ more stable and longer-lived than G2V] |
| Mass | 0.82 M☉ | ← |
| Radius | 0.78 R☉ | ← |
| Luminosity | 0.41 L☉ | ← |
| Photosphere temperature | 5,050 K | ← |
| Age | 6.5 billion years | [⚠ ~2 billion years older than the Sun] |
| Expected main sequence lifetime | ~22 billion years | ← |
| Metallicity ([Fe/H]) | +0.12 | Heavy element excess |
| Activity | Low | Quiet star without strong flares |
| Rotation period | 32.4 standard days | ← |

**Rationale:** The K2V orange dwarf was chosen as an optimal compromise. Compared to a G2V yellow dwarf, Khar'Vex:
- Has a 2.5× longer main-sequence lifetime
- Radiates predominantly in the orange-red spectrum (peak ~570 nm), which influenced the evolution of Xha'Ruun photosynthesis and vision
- Demonstrates greater luminosity stability (fewer variations)
- Its habitable zone is closer to the star (0.5–0.8 AU)
