# Volume IV: Language — Chapter 6: Evidentiality

> *"In the Xha'Ruun language, you cannot simply say 'it is raining.' You must say 'I see that it is raining,' or 'I was told that it is raining,' or 'it appears to be raining.' Truth is not a fact. Truth is a source."*
> — Academic of Semantics, Khal-Ven

## 6.1 Four Types of Evidentiality

| Type | Suffix | Meaning | Example |
|------|--------|---------|---------|
| Direct | -ven | I see/hear it myself | **khal-en-ven** — "does (I see)" |
| Indirect | -sha | I feel/hear it | **khal-en-sha** — "does (I hear)" |
| Inferential | -tor | I draw a conclusion | **khal-en-tor** — "must be doing" |
| Reportative | -rass | I was told | **khal-en-rass** — "they say (he) does" |

## 6.2 Obligatoriness

Evidentiality is mandatory in every utterance. Omitting evidentiality is a grammatical error.
