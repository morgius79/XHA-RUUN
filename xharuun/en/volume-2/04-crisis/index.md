## 4.1 Causes of the Fall

The First Empire collapsed as a result of a combination of several factors that had been accumulating for centuries.

### 4.1.1 Ecological Collapse

The main cause of the fall. Intensive agriculture on the Central Plain of Kharak without crop rotation led to:

- Soil depletion (loss of 40% of humus over 1,000 years)
- Salinization of irrigated lands
- A 60% decline in crop yields
- The famine of −2,150, which claimed ~1 million lives

### 4.1.2 Climate Shift

A cooling of −2.5°C that lasted ~200 years (∼2,250 – −2,050). Caused by a series of volcanic winters.

| Year | Event | T drop | Consequences |
|------|-------|--------|--------------|
| −2,220 | Eruption of Tor-6 | −1.5°C | Crop failure for 3 years |
| −2,180 | Eruption of Raal-1 | −2.8°C | Famine, epidemics |
| −2,150 | Crisis peak | −3.0°C | Fall of the Empire |

### 4.1.3 Social Factors

- Gap between the elite (2%) and the population
- Corruption of the imperial bureaucracy
- Tax increases as crop yields fell
- Slave and peasant uprisings (−2,180, −2,160)

## 4.2 The Fall (−2,150)

Year of the Empire's fall: −2,150. Events unfolded rapidly:

1. **Spring −2,150:** Hunger riot in the capital Verksar
2. **Summer −2,150:** Emperor Verkmiraen V flees south
3. **Autumn −2,150:** Verksar destroyed by rebels
4. **Winter −2,150:** The Empire fractures into 24 independent states

### 4.2.1 Fate of the Dynasty

The last emperor, Verkmiraen V, was killed while attempting to flee. None of his heirs were able to restore power.

---

## 4.3 The Dark Ages (−2,150 – −750)

A period of decline lasting 1,400 years.

### 4.3.1 Demographic Decline

The population of Kharak fell from 18 million to 4 million (−75%).

### 4.3.2 Loss of Knowledge

- Writing survived in only 40% of regions
- The Imperial Code was lost for 600 years
- Metallurgy degraded to bronze
- Trade between continents ceased

### 4.3.3 Centers of Preservation

Knowledge was preserved in religious centers and monasteries:

| Center | Region | Preserved Knowledge |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| Temple of Thar | Thar Valley | Writing, astronomy |
| Ven Cloister | Serrin Plateau | Medicine, botany |
| Library of Irkhen | Irkhen Island | The Imperial Code |
| School of Raal | Raal Plateau | Metallurgy |

---

## 4.4 Recovery (−1,500 – −750)

Gradual emergence from the crisis:

| Period | Process |
|--------|---------|
| −1,500 – −1,200 | Recovery of agriculture |
| −1,200 – −900 | Resumption of interregional trade |
| −900 – −750 | Unification of regions into alliances |

By −800, 8 large regional alliances had formed on Kharak, which later became the foundation of the Treaty of Unity.

## Chapter Summary

1. The First Empire fell in −2,150 due to ecological collapse, climate, and social factors.
2. A cooling of −2.5°C over 200 years exacerbated the crisis.
3. The Dark Ages lasted 1,400 years.
4. The population of Kharak declined by 75% (from 18 million to 4 million).
5. Knowledge was preserved in 5 religious centers.
6. By −750, 8 regional alliances had formed.
