## Introduction

Xha'Ruun (full form: **Xha'Ruun-Vokh** — "thinking people of great harmony") are the only known sapient species on Thexar and in the entire galaxy. They are hexapodal (six-limbed) egg-laying vertebrates with trinocular vision, tetrachromatic color perception, quadraaural hearing, and a triple gender system — one of the most complex reproductive strategies among known life forms.

Xha'Ruun are a product of 4 billion cycles of evolution in unique conditions: under an orange sky, at lower gravity, with sulfur-rich biochemistry and elevated background radiation. Every feature of their anatomy and physiology is a response to the challenges of the Thexar environment.

## 5.1 Evolutionary Origin

### 5.1.1 Genealogy

Xha'Ruun belong to the class **Thermochromadae** ("heat-colors") — an endemic vertebrate class of Thexar, characterized by:
- Exo-endoskeletal combination (chitinous outer layer + bony internal skeleton)
- Hexapodal symmetry (6 limbs)
- Double circulatory circuit (2 hearts)
- Cutaneous thermoregulation through chromatophore-heliotherms
- Egg-laying reproduction

> **TERM: Thermochromadae**
>
> The class of animals to which Xha'Ruun belong. The name derives from the roots *thermo* (heat) and *chroma* (color), reflecting the class members' ability to regulate body temperature through changes in skin pigmentation that absorb radiation. The class includes ~24,000 species on Thexar — from small reptile-like forms (0.24 sha) to large herbivores.

### 5.2.2 Limbs

**Six limbs** of Xha'Ruun are divided into three functional pairs:

| Pair | Name | Function | Fingers | Feature |
|------|------|----------|---------|---------|
| 1 (lower) | **Legs** (khar-gran) | Locomotion, support | 4 (3+1 opposable) | Clawed foot |

| 3 (upper) | **Vexes** (vex-gran) | Manipulation + gliding | 3 + claw | **Wing-arms** |

> **KEY FACT**
>
> The upper pair of limbs (vexes) is a unique evolutionary adaptation. Originally they were wings for gliding flight (during the arboreal life era). They subsequently reduced in size but retained the ability to glide short distances (up to 61 sha) and acquired manipulator functions. In adult Xha'Ruun, vexes are used for:
> - Balance support during running
> - Gliding when falling from height (emergency function)
> - Gesticulation (additional communication channel)
> - Fine work (requiring 3 fingers)
> - Social rituals (embraces, greetings)

## 5.3 Internal Organs and Systems

### 5.3.1 Circulatory System

Xha'Ruun have a **closed two-circuit circulatory system with two hearts**:

| Heart | Location | Function | Resting Rhythm |
|-------|----------|----------|----------------|
| **Thoracic heart** (ven-thor) | Upper sternum | Pumps blood to head and upper limbs | 35–50 bpm |
| **Abdominal heart** (ven-abd) | Center of abdominal cavity | Pumps blood to lower body and internal organs | 28–42 bpm |

The hearts work in **alternating phase**: when the thoracic heart contracts, the abdominal one relaxes, and vice versa. This creates a constant, pulsating blood flow without "dead phases."

**Xha'Ruun blood** is blue-green in color due to hemocyanin (a copper-containing pigment, analogous to hemoglobin), which is characteristic of many species of the class Thermochromadae.

| Parameter | Xha'Ruun |
|-----------|----------|
| Number of hearts | **2** |
| Blood pigment | Hemocyanin (Cu) |
| Blood color | Blue-green |
| Number of lungs | **4** |
| Blood pressure (sys.) | 170/100 |
| Blood volume | ~12 L |

### 5.3.2 Respiratory System

The four lungs of Xha'Ruun are arranged in pairs:
- **Upper pair** (khel-lung) — responsible for basal breathing (always active)
- **Lower pair** (thar-lung) — engages during physical exertion (reserve capacity)

Total lung capacity: ~14 L.
Resting respiratory rate: 8–12 breaths/min.

**Supplementary breathing:** Xha'Ruun skin contains a capillary network capable of absorbing up to 8% of oxygen (important during extended submersion).

### 5.3.3 Digestive System

Xha'Ruun are **omnivorous**, with a bias toward a protein-plant diet. The digestive system is adapted to the high sulfur content in food (→ see [Ch.04, "Biochemistry"]):

| Organ | Function | Feature |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Oral cavity | Grinding | 3 rows of teeth: cutting, crushing, grinding |
| Esophagus | Transport | Length ~1.5 sha |
| Stomach (1st) | Acid digestion | pH 1.5–2.0 |
| Stomach (2nd) | Alkaline digestion | pH 8.0–8.5 |
| Midgut | Absorption | Length ~7.3 sha |
| Cecum | Sulfur processing | Enlarged |
| Hindgut | Water exchange | — |

> **KEY FACT**
>
> Xha'Ruun possess a **second nerve cluster** — the abdominal ganglion, often called the "second brain" or "intuitive brain." It manages autonomic functions (digestion, breathing, heartbeat) and, according to Xha'Ruun neuroscientists, participates in processing emotions and intuitive decisions. The expression "deciding with the belly" (thar-ven) is not a metaphor but a physiological reality.

### 5.4.1 Vision

Xha'Ruun are **tetrachromats** with four types of cones in the retina:

| Cone Type | Sensitivity Peak | Color | Range |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|-------|
| S (short) | 380 nm | Ultraviolet | 320–450 nm |
| M1 (medium 1) | 490 nm | Blue | 450–540 nm |
| M2 (medium 2) | **580 nm** | **Orange** (peak) | 530–650 nm |
| L (long) | 680 nm | Red | 620–780 nm |

**Vision features:**
- **UV channel** (320–450 nm) — allows seeing patterns on flowers, traces of pheromones, distinguishing substances invisible to other visual systems
- **Trinocular depth** — 3 eyes provide redundant stereo vision with high distance estimation accuracy
- **Night vision:** rods predominate in the retina

### 5.4.2 Hearing

The four ears of Xha'Ruun provide **quadraaural perception** — the ability to determine sound direction with ±0.5° accuracy:

| Ear Pair | Location | Directionality | Range |
|----------|----------|----------------|-------|
| Front | On sides of head, forward | Frontal | 20 Hz – 42 kHz |
| Rear | On sides of skull, rearward | Rear | **8 Hz** – 20 kHz |

**Infrasound sensitivity** (8–20 Hz) — an evolutionary inheritance: allows hearing approaching earthquakes, volcanic activity, movement of large animals from tens of mo away. In modern civilization it is used in musical instruments and architectural acoustics (→ see [Ch.10, "Resonance Temples"]).

### 5.4.3 Smell

The Xha'Ruun olfactory system is one of the most developed among sapient species. ~1,500 receptor types allow:

| Category | Recognizable Scents | Example |
|----------|---------------------|---------|
| Biological | Pheromones (8 main types) | Fear, aggression, sexual readiness |
| Food-related | Freshness, toxicity, ripeness | — |
| Environmental | Approaching storms, air humidity | — |
| Social | Individual body odor | Identification with 90% accuracy |

**Pheromone communication** is an important aspect of social life (→ see [Ch.08, "Culture"]).

### 5.4.4 Electroreception

Specialized cells (Khara ampullae) in the skin of the Xha'Ruun head allow sensing weak electric fields with intensity from 0.1 µV/cm.

| Application | Sensitivity | Range |
|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Detection of living beings | Heartbeat | up to 18 sha |
| Navigation in murky water | Geomagnetic anomalies | up to 122 sha |
| Weather assessment | Air electrification (storms) | up to 6 mo |

In modern Xha'Ruun, electroreception is a **rudimentary organ**, significantly less acute than in the ancestral state. It is mainly used for sensing weather.

### 5.4.5 Thermoreception

Infrared sensors located on the upper pair of limbs (vexes) allow sensing thermal radiation in the range of 45–100°X with ±0.3°X accuracy. Evolutionarily, this was a hunting tool for tracking warm-blooded prey.

In modern society it is used for:
- Medical diagnostics (inflammations, tumors)
- Ambient temperature assessment
- Social interaction (embraces — sensing body warmth)

## 5.5 Reproductive System and Reproduction

### 5.5.1 Three Genders

> **KEY FACT**
>
> Xha'Ruun are one of the few known sapient species with a **triple-gender reproductive system**. Three genders — **male (♂), female (♀), and neuter (⚲)** — perform different biological and social functions, and all three are necessary for population reproduction.

| Gender | Genotype | Gametes | Population Share | Reproductive Role |
|--------|----------|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Male (♂) | XY | Sperm | ~15% | Fertilization |
| Female (♀) | XX | Eggs | ~20% | Egg production |
| Neuter (⚲) | XN | **None** | ~65% | **Social** — sterile |

**Biological rationale for three genders:**
- Males and females produce gametes (haploid)
- The XN genotype (neuter) results from a particular combination of alleles that blocks gonad development
- The high proportion of neuters (65%) is an evolutionary strategy: "worker individuals" who do not divert resources to reproduction dedicate their lives to labor, science, and creativity

### 5.5.2 Reproductive Cycle

| Stage | Duration | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Courtship | 1–3 years | Formation of a triad (♂+♀+⚲) |
| Mating | 1 time | Male fertilizes female |
| Egg formation | 60–70 days | Inside the female's body |
| Laying | 1–2 days | 2–4 eggs |
| Incubation (external) | 180–200 days | At t = 60…63°X |
| Hatching | — | Larva-like stage |
| Rearing | 2–3 years | All 3 parents participate |
| Sexual maturity | ~25–30 years | — |

> **TERM: Triad**
>
> The basic reproductive unit of Xha'Ruun is the triad (one male + one female + one or two neuters). The triad forms a family, jointly raises offspring, and runs a household. Neuters in the triad are sterile partners fulfilling the roles of providers, builders, and educators. Social bonds in the triad are not biological but emotional and social.

### 5.5.4 Lifespan

The average lifespan of Xha'Ruun is **150–200 years**. Recorded maximum: 247 years (Ven-Thal, Keeper of the First Library, +2,890 – +3,137 E.U.).

1. Efficient DNA repair mechanisms (→ see [Ch.04, "Radiation Resistance"])

3. Diet rich in antioxidants (selenium compounds)
4. Low stress levels (social support of the triad + lack of competition for food)

## 5.7 Thermoregulation

Xha'Ruun are **endothermic** (warm-blooded) organisms with elements of heliothermy (heat absorption through the skin).

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Body temperature (resting) | 65.3°X |
| Perspiration | Low |
| Shivering | Absent |
| Solar heating | Yes (up to +1.1°X) |
| Activity peak | Morning + evening |

Heliotherm skin cells contain silicon-carbon channels (→ see [Ch.04, "Si-channels"]) that absorb infrared radiation and convert it into heat. After 20–30 minutes in the sun, a Xha'Ruun can raise its body temperature by 1–2°C (0.5–1.1°X), saving energy on metabolic thermogenesis.

### 5.8.2 Social Intelligence

Xha'Ruun are a highly social species. Isolation is psychologically destructive for them. Main manifestations of social intelligence:

- **Second-order empathy** — ability to automatically assess a tribesperson's emotional state through pheromonal scent
- **Collective memory** — tendency to transfer communal memories to shared archives
- **Ritualization of conflicts** — disputes rarely escalate to violence (the triad and guild structure provides mediation)

### 5.8.3 Emotional Range

Xha'Ruun emotions can be amplified by pheromones (their own and others'). In an enclosed space without ventilation, a group of Xha'Ruun can enter **emotional resonance** — a state where emotions synchronize through the pheromonal channel, leading to group euphoria or panic.
