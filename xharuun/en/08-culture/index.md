## Introduction

Xha'Ruun culture is a product of 12,000 years of social evolution, in which biological imperatives (three genders, pheromone communication, clan instinct) and historical experience (rise and fall of the Empire, epidemic, rebirth) are intertwined. This is a culture where **collective knowledge** is valued above individual ambitions, **truth and its source** above gain, and **harmony** above conflict.

Xha'Ruun society is one of the most stable in the known galaxy. The Council of Unity has existed for 3,178 years without interruption. Guilds are not merely professional associations but bearers of an ethical code. Triads are not merely families but cells of mutual aid.

### 8.1.2 Castes vs Guilds

Formally, there is no caste system. De facto, society is stratified by profession:

| Level | Criteria | Approximate Share |
|-------|----------|-------------------|
| **First Voice** | Chair of the Council | 1 |
| **Keepers** (Vek'ruun) | Council members, guild heads | ~0.01% |
| **Masters** (Sha'ruun) | Highest qualification, academic degrees | ~5% |
| **Artisans** (Thar'ruun) | Professional certification | ~45% |
| **Students** (Xa'ruun) | In training, entry level | ~20% |
| **Apprentices** (Mor'ruun) | Basic education, unskilled labor | ~30% |

**Social mobility:** high. Any Xha'Ruun can rise from Student to Master by passing a series of professional exams and obtaining recommendations from current masters. There are no "class barriers."

## 8.3 Ethics and Values

### 8.3.1 Three Pillars of Xha'Ruun Ethics

| Value | Concept | Meaning |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **Knowledge** | **Ven** | Highest value. Knowledge is not power, it is responsibility. Sharing knowledge is a moral duty. Concealing knowledge is a crime. |
| **Harmony** | **Rass** | Balance between personal and public, tradition and progress, labor and rest. |
| **Truth** | **Ven-Ash** | Truthfulness as the foundation of trust. Lies destroy social bonds (cf. obligatory evidentiality in language, → see [Ch.06, "Evidentiality"]). |

### 8.3.2 Taboos and Prohibitions

| Action | Status | Punishment |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Willful destruction of knowledge | **Gravest** | Exile from guilds |
| Deliberate lie with direct evidentiality | **Grave** | Up to 20 years correctional labor |
| Birth suppression (without medical indication) | **Grave** | Public censure |
| Killing a tribesperson | **Gravest** | Life imprisonment / execution |
| Harm to a child/larva | **Gravest** | Life sentence |
| Theft of knowledge (plagiarism) | **Grave** | Loss of guild status |
| Violation of guild oaths | **Moderate** | Expulsion from guild |

### 8.3.3 Attitude Toward Knowledge

Knowledge in Xha'Ruun culture is not merely power. It is a **sacred duty** to past and future generations.

> **KEY FACT**
>
> Xha'Ruun have a concept of **"Debt of Knowledge"** (ven-mor) — the moral obligation of every member of society to share their knowledge with the archives. Refusal to transmit knowledge (for example, a scientist taking a discovery to the grave) is considered nearly worse than murder. "To kill a body is to deprive one life. To kill knowledge is to deprive thousands of lives that could have used it."

## 8.4 Triad and Family

### 8.4.1 Triad

> **TERM: Triad**
>
> The basic family-economic unit of Xha'Ruun. Consists of 3–4 individuals: one male (♂), one female (♀), and one or two neuters (⚲). The triad is not necessarily based on biological kinship — it is a social contract sealed by the ceremony of **"Merging of Voices"** (sha-thar).

| Triad Member | Role | Reproductive Function |
|--------------|------|------------------------|
| Male (♂) | Provision, external relations | Fertilization |
| Female (♀) | Management, hierarchy | Egg laying |
| Neuter(s) (⚲) | Upbringing, household | Sterile |

**Triad formation** is voluntary, usually at age 25–40, after a courtship period (1–3 years). The triad is registered with the guild of Scribes.

### 8.4.2 Family Life Cycle

| Stage | Duration | Event |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Courtship | 1–3 years | Acquaintance, joint work |
| Triad formation | Ceremony | "Merging of Voices" |
| First clutch | 2–5 years after | 2–4 eggs |
| Rearing | 25+ years | Until offspring independence |
| Post-reproductive | 50+ years | Mentorship |
| Triad dissolution | Upon members' death | — |

### 8.4.3 Child Rearing

Xha'Ruun children (larvae) hatch from eggs after 180–200 days of incubation. The first 3 years of life is a larva-like stage requiring constant care (fed with a milk-like secretion produced by the skin of neuters). All three members of the triad participate in upbringing.

Education begins at age 3: language, social norms, basic skills. From age 12 — professional training in guilds.

## 8.5 Art and Literature

### 8.5.1 Aesthetic Principles

Xha'Ruun art is based on three aesthetic principles:

1. **Geometric order** — symmetry, repeating patterns, fractal structures (reflecting the senary system and love of order)
2. **Resonance** — art must "sound" (even visual art) — evoke an emotional response, synchronize with the viewer's pheromonal background
3. **Transparency** — art should not hide the process of creation; the structure is visible, the layers are legible

### 8.5.2 Music

Music holds a special place in Xha'Ruun culture — it is directly connected to religion (→ see [Ch.10, "Religion"]) and architectural acoustics.

| Genre | Description | Typical Instrument |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Sacred** | Multi-frequency chorales for 2 pairs of vocal cords | Live voice (only) |
| **Chamber** | Instrumental ensembles (2–6) | Khar-long (string), Thar-drum |
| **Symphonic** | Orchestra (up to 60) | Full Xha'Ruun orchestra |
| **Electronic** | Frequency synthesizers | Quantrophone |

**Unique instrument — quantrophone:** produces sound from quantum current fluctuations in a superconductor. Its sound is believed to be the closest to the "voice of the universe" (the Primordial Vibration).

**Music theory** is based on the **senary system**:
- **6 notes** in an octave
- Intervals: prime, fifth, fourth, third (6-step scale)
- Modes: 6 main (major-like, minor-like, "twilight," "dawn," "cosmic," "temple")
- Rhythms: based on the number 6 — time signatures 6/8, 6/4, 12/8

**Historical periods of music:**

| Period | Dates | Characteristic | Notable Composer |
|--------|-------|---------------|------------------|
| **Archaic** | −5,000 – −3,000 | Monophonic hymns, ritual drums | Anonymous |
| **Classical** | −3,000 – −2,100 | Development of choral music, first instrumental ensembles | Thar-Vex-Mor (~−2,800) |
| **Post-crisis** | −1,500 – −500 | Simplification, folk motifs, revival | Shan-Vek-Khar (~−1,200) |
| **Romantic** | −500 – +500 | Symphony orchestra, program music | Ruun-Sha-En (~−200) |
| **Modern** | +500 – present | Electronic, quantrophone, genre synthesis | Vex-Thar-Nur (+3,100) |

### 8.5.3 Literature

The Xha'Ruun literary tradition spans ~10,000 years. The key genre is the **chronicle** (historical narrative with obligatory evidentiality).

> **TABLE: Genres of Xha'Ruun Literature**
>
> | Genre | Characteristic | Evidentiality | Example |
> |-------|---------------|---------------|---------|
> | **Chronicles** | Historical records | Obligatory | "Chronicles of Eternity" (12 volumes) |
> | **Treatises** | Scientific-philosophical | High | "Grammar of Being" (+1,200) |
> | **Epic** | Heroic tales | Partial | "Song of the Seven Voices" |
> | **Poetry** | Strict metrical forms | Optional | "Cycle of Times" (Sha-En-Vex) |
> | **Drama** | Theater with music | Low | "Fall of the Empire" |
> | **Prose** | Novels, stories | Variable | "Journey to Xhel" |

**Great works:**

1. **"Chronicles of Eternity"** (Mor-Vokh-Sha) — 12-volume historical work covering history from −12,000 to +2,000 y.c. Compiled over 800 years by several generations of Keepers.
2. **"Grammar of Being"** (Sha-Ven-Thex) — philosophical treatise by Master Shaen-Nur, describing the connection between language, thought, and reality.
3. **"Song of the Seven Voices"** (Khar-Vokh-Shan) — epic poem in 108 cantos, each dedicated to one aspect of cosmogony.
4. **"Cycle of Times"** (Mor-Shan) — poetry cycle by poet Sha-En-Vex, exploring the nature of time and memory.

**Xha'Ruun poetic form** is built on syllabic meter (not tonic). The basic meter is **hexameter** (6 syllables per line, with caesura after the 3rd syllable):

```
khar-ven  /  the-xa-mor
"light-truth / earth-life-time"
```

More complex forms use 6 × n syllables with rhyme on the 1st and 4th stressed syllable of the line.

### 8.5.4 Architecture

Xha'Ruun architecture is characterized by:
- **Hexagonal forms** (hexagons, hexahedra) — from city plans to ornaments
- **Acoustic design** — buildings are designed with sound resonance in mind
- **Horizontality** — due to low population density, cities grow outward rather than upward (skyscrapers are rare)
- **Environmental integration** — buildings do not dominate the landscape but blend into it

## 8.9 Communication and Etiquette

### 8.9.1 Verbal Communication

Due to pheromone sensitivity (→ see [Ch.05, "Smell"]), Xha'Ruun communication includes more than just words:
- **Words:** 60% of information
- **Intonation + ultrasound:** 25% of information (emotional background)
- **Pheromones:** 15% of information (status, aggression, trust)
- **Vex gestures:** up to 10% (additional channel)

### 8.9.2 Etiquette

| Situation | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| Greeting | Press palms of middle hands together + slight head bow |
| Respect for elder | Turn vexes toward the interlocutor (display of openness) |
| Gratitude | Touch chest (area of the thoracic heart) |
| Apology | Draw vexes back (display of vulnerability) |
| Disagreement | Turn rear ears away from the interlocutor |
| Mourning | Rear ears pressed tightly to the head |

### 8.9.3 Personal Space

Xha'Ruun personal distance:
- **Intimate:** <0.6 sha (only triad members)
- **Personal:** 0.6–1.8 sha (friends, colleagues)
- **Social:** 1.8–4.9 sha (formal communication)
- **Public:** >4.9 sha (speeches)

## 8.10 Cuisine

### 8.10.1 Diet

Xha'Ruun are omnivorous, with a protein-plant diet.

| Food Type | Share | Examples |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Grains (analog) | 40% | Thar-rice, khar-millet |
| Animal protein | 25% | Kharru meat, fish |
| Vegetables/fruit | 25% | Wide variety (~80 species) |
| Spices and herbs | 5% | Selen-grass, fermented leaves |
| H₂S-containing | 5% | Sulfur-beans, mineral supplements |

### 8.10.2 Culinary Traditions

- Food preparation is a ritual, no less significant than consumption
- Fermentation is widespread (~40% of foods undergo fermentation)
- Spicy and sour flavors are preferred over sweet
- Salt (NaCl) is used but less popular than selenium salt (Se-salt)
