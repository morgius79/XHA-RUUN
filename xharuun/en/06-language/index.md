## Introduction

The language of Xha'Ruun (endonym: **Sha'Ruun** /ʃɑˈruːn/ — "language of the people") is one of the oldest continuously used languages. Its written history spans nearly 10,000 years, and its oral tradition extends tens of millennia into the past.

Sha'Ruun is an agglutinative language with a basic SOV word order, 14 cases, obligatory evidentiality, and a unique writing system combining syllabic signs (syllabary) and logograms. Its phonetics reflect Xha'Ruun anatomy — 4 ears and trinocular vision have shaped the fine structure of prosody.

This chapter is not merely a grammatical sketch. It is a portrait of a language that shaped a civilization, and a civilization that shaped a language.

### 6.1.3 Prosody

| Parameter | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| **Stress** | Dynamic, falls on the **first syllable of the root** |
| **Length** | Phonemic (distinguishes words: **khar** /xar/ — "light", **khaar** /xaːr/ — "radiance") |
| **Tone** | Absent (not a tonal language) |
| **Intonation** | Falling (declarative), rising (question), level (subordinate clauses) |
| **Rhythm** | Syllable-timed (all syllables have approximately equal duration) |

> **KEY FACT**
>
> The four ears of Xha'Ruun (→ see [Ch.05, "Hearing"]) allow them to distinguish **ultrasonic components of speech** (up to 42 kHz) that are inaccessible to many other auditory systems. Standard Sha'Ruun has no phonemic ultrasonic contrasts, but they exist in some dialects and poetic forms.

### 6.1.4 Phonotactics

| Rule | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Syllable structure** | (C)(C)V(C)(C) | **thrak** (valid), **skhar** (valid) |
| **Onsets** | plosive + liquid (pr, tr, kr), fricative + nasal (fn, sn) | **pral**, **fnar** |
| **Codas** | -n, -r, -l, -s, -sh, -k, -t | **khar**, **ven**, **thal** |
| **Prohibited** | 3+ consonants at onset; voiced+voiceless heterogeneous pair | — |

## 6.2 Writing System

### 6.2.1 General Description

The Xha'Ruun writing system is **mixed**: syllabary + logograms. It has existed in this form for ~8,000 years, having evolved from pictographic proto-writing to the modern system.

> **PROFILE: Sha'Ruun Writing System**
>
> | Parameter | Value |
> |-----------|-------|
> | Type | **Syllabo-logographic** |
> | Number of signs | 124 syllabic + **48 logograms** |
> | Direction | **Right to left**, rows top to bottom |
> | Age | ~8,000 years (modern form) |
> | Origin | Pictographic proto-writing (~−9,800 y.c.) |
> | Logogram scope | Fundamental concepts, deities, numbers |

### 6.2.2 Writing Direction

```
     SHA'RUUN WRITING DIRECTION

  ┌──────────────────────────────────────┐

  │  [♁][◇][▼]  [☰][◆][↑]  [▽][♁][◈]  │  <── row 2
  │  [↑][♁][☰]  [◆][▽][♁]  [▼][◈][↑]  │  <── row 3
  │                                      │
  │  ←──── READ RIGHT TO LEFT ──────────→ │
```

### 6.2.5 Numerals

Xha'Ruun use a **senary numeral system** (base 6). Counting is from 0 to 5, places are powers of 6.

| Digit | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|-------|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sign | ○ | ● | ⬕ | ◧ | ◪ | ⬠ |

**Examples:**
- 6₁₀ = 10₆ = ●○
- 42₁₀ = 1×36 + 1×6 + 0 = 110₆ = ●●○
- 100₁₀ = 2×36 + 4×6 + 4 = 244₆ = ◧◪◪

> **RELATION**

### 6.3.4 Verbal Morphology

The verb in Sha'Ruun is the central element of the sentence. It agglutinates up to 8 categories:

```
Structure of the verb chain (left to right):

[ROOT] — [ASPECT] — [TENSE] — [MOOD] — [VOICE] — [POLARITY] — [EVID.] — [SUBJECT]
```

**Aspect:** 4 aspects — perfective (-ash), imperfective (-an), punctual (-at), iterative (-ur)

**Tense:** 3+1 tenses — past (-en), present (-∅), future (-el) + timeless (-eth)

**Mood:** indicative, subjunctive, optative, imperative, presumptive

**Voice:** active, passive (-aθ), causative (-es), medial (-ol)

**Polarity:** affirmative (∅), negative (-na)

**Evidentiality** (obligatory category!):

| Marker | Evidence Type | Example | Translation |
|--------|---------------|---------|-------------|
| -sha | Direct (saw/heard) | **ven-sha** | know because I saw |
| -mor | Indirect (was told) | **ven-mor** | know because I was told |
| -zal | Inferential (deduction) | **ven-zal** | must be so (I assume) |
| -thar | Reportative (citation) | **ven-thar** | they say that... |

> **KEY FACT**
>
> **Evidentiality is obligatory.** In Sha'Ruun, one cannot simply say "he came" — one must indicate how one knows: whether one saw it oneself (**khel-en-sha**), was told (**khel-en-mor**), deduced it (**khel-en-zal**), or it is common knowledge (**khel-en-eth**). Speaking without an evidentiality marker is grammatically incorrect. This has left its mark on culture: Xha'Ruun value precise sources of knowledge (→ see [Ch.08, "Ethics"]).

**Subject agreement** — the final element of the chain:

| Subject | Suffix |
|---------|--------|
| 1st person | -ve (I), -vek (I+you, we two), -vet (we all) |
| 2nd person | -en (you sg.), -ent (you pl.) |
| 3rd person | -un (he/she/it), -unt (they) |

**Example of a complete verb chain:**

```
Khar-en   xha-ash-el-na-zal-ve
Light-ERG think-PFV-FUT-NEG-INFR-1SG
"I probably will not think (that way) (I have concluded that I will not think)"
```

## 6.4 Syntax

### 6.4.1 Word Order

Basic word order: **SOV** (Subject — Object — Verb)

```
Xha'Ruun-en   ven-ar     sha-el
Person-ERG    truth-ACC   speak-FUT
"Xha'Ruun will speak the truth"
```

### 6.4.2 Complex Sentences

| Type | Conjunction | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Coordination | -iv (and), -mor (but), -ghal (or) | **ven-iv thar** "truth and strength" |
| Reason | thel (because) | ...**thel** khar-en ven-sha "because the star spoke truth" |
| Purpose | vex (in order to) | ...**vex** ruun-ol ven "so that the people may know" |
| Condition | zhal-esh (if) | ...**zhal-esh** khar-el "if it shines" |

### 6.5.2 Word Formation

> **IMPORTANT**
>
> Word formation in Sha'Ruun is **compounding** (root combination) and **derivational** (suffixes). The meaning of a compound word is usually transparent: **khar-vex** (light + to give) = "star that gives light." This allows speakers to understand new words intuitively.

**Compound word models:**

| Model | Structure | Example | Literal | Translation |
|-------|-----------|---------|---------|-------------|
| Attributive | N+N | **khar-ven** | light-truth | "enlightenment" |
| Verbal | V+N | **sha-ven** | speak-truth | "to proclaim" |
| Qualifying | Adj+N | **khel-thar** | sky-strength | "energy of the sky" |
| Causal | N+V | **thar-xa** | strength-life | "energy of life" |
| Contrastive | N+Neg | **ven-na** | truth-no | "falsehood" |

### 6.5.3 Proper Names

**Xha'Ruun personal names** are usually bipartite — two roots joined by a hyphen:

| Name | First Root | Second Root | Meaning |
|------|------------|-------------|---------|
| **Khar-Vex** | khar- (light) | vex- (to give) | "Light-Giver" |
| **Thar-Mor** | thar- (strength) | mor- (time) | "Strength of Time" |
| **Sha-En** | sha- (language) | en- (truth/high) | "Language of Truth" |
| **Ven-Thal** | ven- (truth) | thal- (river) | "River of Truth" |
| **Rhaal-Ven** | rhaal- (search) | ven- (truth) | "Seeker of Truth" |
| **Thash-Ven** | thash- (depth) | ven- (truth) | "Deep Truth" |

**Naming conventions:**
- Stress falls on the second part of the compound (unlike ordinary words, where stress is on the first syllable of the root)
- Female names often end in **-a**, **-ae** (Vex-a, Shaen-ae)
- Male names often end in **-en**, **-un** (Thar-mor-en, Rhaal-ven-un)
- Neuter names often end in **-in**, **-el** (Rhaalin, Venel)
- The honorific suffix **-vek** (wisdom) is added to the name of a spiritual or scientific authority

## 6.6 Evidentiality and Culture

Obligatory evidentiality in Sha'Ruun is not a grammatical curiosity but a reflection of a fundamental cultural value: **respect for the source of knowledge**.

| Evidentiality | Social Function |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Direct (-sha) | "I am a witness" — high reliability |
| Indirect (-mor) | "I was told" — source attribution |
| Inferential (-zal) | "I suppose" — intellectual honesty |
| Reportative (-thar) | "So they say" — distancing from unreliable information |

A lie with the **-sha** marker (direct evidence) is considered the gravest offense — it is not just deception but distortion of testimony. A lie with **-thar** (reportative) is less serious ("I was told so, I am relaying it").

## 6.8 Language and Biology

### 6.8.1 Articulatory Basis

The Xha'Ruun speech apparatus includes:

| Organ | Xha'Ruun | Phonetic Consequences |
|-------|----------|-----------------------|
| Vocal cords | **2 pairs** | Ability to produce two tones simultaneously (used in sacred singing) |
| Tongue | Mobile, **~18 sha** | Broad articulatory range |
| Airflow | **4 lungs** (→ see [Ch.05]) | Longer phrases without pauses |
| Resonators | Mouth + nose + **additional sinuses** | Timbre rich in overtones |

### 6.8.2 Language and Perception

The four ears of Xha'Ruun did not add phonemes but created:
- **Tonal sensitivity** — ability to distinguish speech overtones carrying emotional information
- **Ultrasonic articulation** — possible but not used in standard language (employed in religious rituals)
- **Localization redundancy** — ability to understand speech in noisy environments

### 6.9.2 Loanwords

Sha'Ruun actively borrows words from regional dialects during eras of political unity. Foreign loanwords (from languages of other clans) are adapted to phonotactics: clusters are simplified, final consonants are added.
