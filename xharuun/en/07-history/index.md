## Introduction

The history of Xha'Ruun civilization spans ~12,000 years — from the first permanent settlements in the fertile Thar Valley to modern space-faring society. Over this time, civilization passed through the greatest rises and deepest falls: continental unification and ecological collapse, a flourishing of sciences and Dark Ages, the invention of writing and the expansion into space.

Xha'Ruun history is not linear progress. It is more of a spiral: each successive generation stands on the shoulders of the previous ones, but some shoulders were broken by wars and catastrophes. And yet — civilization survived, preserved knowledge, and continues to develop.

## 7.2 Pre-civilization Era (−12,000 – −9,800)

### 7.2.1 Origin

The first archaeological traces of Xha'Ruun ancestors date to ~−15,000 y.c. — stone tools (hand axes, scrapers) in the Thar Valley region (continent Kharak). The hominid lineage of Thermochromadae by this time already possessed:
- Bipedalism (~−150,000 years)
- Enlarged brain (~−80,000 years)
- Proto-language (~−20,000 years)
- Control of fire (~−5,000 years)

### 7.2.2 First Permanent Settlements

**~−11,500 y.c.** — the first permanent settlements appear on the terraces of the Khar-Tor river. The oldest known is **Thar-Vex** ("Gift of the Valley," now an archaeological site within the modern city limits).

| Settlement | Region | Dating | Population (est.) | Feature |
|------------|--------|--------|-------------------|---------|
| Thar-Vex | Thar Valley, Kharak | −11,500 | ~200 | Oldest |
| Khar-Ur | Serrin Coast, Kharak | −11,200 | ~150 | Fishing |
| The-Nur | Raal Plateau, Rhuval | −10,800 | ~120 | Highland |

### 7.2.3 Domestication

**~−10,200 y.c.** — domestication of **kharru** (herbivore, analog of large cattle). Kharru is a six-legged animal of the class Thermochromadae, providing meat, milk (protein analog), and hides. Domestication occurred independently in two regions: Thar Valley (Kharak) and Raal Plateau (Rhuval).

### 7.2.4 Proto-Art

Primitive Xha'Ruun left behind cave paintings in the caves of Kharak (~−10,500 y.c.). Depictions include:
- Hunting scenes (hexapodal figures with spears)
- Astronomical symbols (a star with 7 rays — early form of the logogram **khar**)
- Abstract spiral patterns (possibly proto-writing)

> **QUOTE**
>
> "In the cave of Khar-Tor, at a depth of 200 meters, we found what we did not hope to find: a depiction of the sky. Thousands of dots chiseled into stone — a star map 12,000 years old. They looked at the same stars as we do."
>
> — Archaeologist Shaen-Khar, "Cave Observatories of the Ancients," Era of Unity

## 7.3 Early Civilization (−9,800 – −6,200)

### 7.3.1 Invention of Writing

**−9,780 y.c.** — proto-writing appears independently in three regions:
1. **Thar Valley** (Kharak) — proto-syllabary, ~120 signs
2. **Serrin Coast** (Kharak) — pictographic writing, ~80 signs
3. **Raal Plateau** (Rhuval) — knotted writing (quipu-like)

By **−9,200**, the Thar Valley system becomes dominant — its simplicity and efficiency (one sign = one syllable) secured victory over competitors.

### 7.3.2 Metallurgy

**−9,200 y.c.** — first copper tools in the foothills of the Khar-Tor range. Copper was smelted in primitive furnaces at 643°X. By **−8,500** bronze appears (copper + arsenic, later tin).

> **TABLE 7.3: Development of Metallurgy**
>
> | Metal | Date | Region | Application |
> |-------|------|--------|-------------|
> | Copper | −9,200 | Kharak (Khar-Tor) | Tools, ornaments |
> | Bronze (As) | −8,500 | Kharak | Weapons, tools |
> | Bronze (Sn) | −8,100 | Kharak + Rhuval | Standard alloys |
> | Iron | −7,300 | Kharak | Tools, weapons |
> | Steel | −5,800 | Kharak | (Flourishing Era) |

### 7.3.3 City-States

By **−7,800** the first city-states form. Largest:

| City | Continent | Population (peak) | Character |
|------|-----------|------------------|-----------|
| **Khar-Vexar** | Kharak | 18,600 | Trade, political center |
| **Vexan** | Kharak | 12,200 | Religious center |
| **Moraat** | Rhuval | 8,400 | Mountain, metallurgical |
| **Sharrus** | Vexar | 5,100 | Port, fishing |

### 7.3.4 First Laws

**−7,200 y.c.** — "Codex of Khar" — the oldest known written legal code (40 articles carved on a stone stele in Khar-Vexar). Regulates: property, marriage (triads), punishments, debts.

## 7.4 Flourishing Era (−6,200 – −2,100)

> *"In those times, the stars shone brighter, knowledge grew faster, and the peoples sang one song."*
> — Classical chronicle "Century of Light," anonymous

### 7.4.1 Foundation of the First Empire

**−6,150 y.c.** — King **Vexan I** of the Khar-Tor dynasty unites eight city-states of the Thar Valley under a single authority. This date is considered the beginning of the First Empire.

Ruler's title: **"First Voice"** (Khar-Vokh) — symbolically, the ruler spoke "on behalf of all voices" (clans).

### 7.4.4 Architecture and Art

The First Empire left behind monumental structures:

- **First Library** (−4,800 y.c.) — collection of ~200,000 clay tablets and papyrus scrolls
- **Khar-Tor Bridge** (−3,400 y.c.) — stone arch bridge 840 m long

### 7.4.5 Decline

By **−2,500 y.c.** the First Empire shows signs of decline:
- Overpopulation (~1.1 billion by the end of the era)
- Resource depletion (deforestation, soil erosion)
- Climatic changes (little ice age on Thexar)
- Political corruption and separatism

## 7.5 Era of Crisis (−2,100 – −750)

### 7.5.1 Fall of the First Empire

**−2,150 y.c.** — the First Empire falls. The catalyst was the **Three-Year Drought** (−2,152 – −2,149) — the worst drought in 2,000 years, causing mass famine.

**−2,120 y.c.** — epidemic of **"Red Rot"** — a bacterial infection affecting the hemocyanin circulatory system of Xha'Ruun. Mortality: ~30% of the population in the epicenter (Thar Valley). The epidemic claimed an estimated **~200 million lives** in 15 years.

> **KEY FACT**
>
> The Red Rot epidemic of −2,120 – −2,105 was the greatest catastrophe in Xha'Ruun history. It destroyed ~12% of the entire planetary population.

### 7.5.2 Dark Ages (−2,100 – −1,800)

After the fall of the Empire came an era of fragmentation:
- Disintegration into ~200 independent city-states and clan territories
- Loss of centralized knowledge (First Library plundered)
- Technological regression (loss of steel and glass secrets)
- Demographic collapse (population reduced to ~400 million)
- Resource wars between clans

#### 7.5.2.1 Clan War (−2,100 – −1,950)

**Key conflict of the Dark Ages** — a prolonged series of wars known as the **"Clan War"** (Ghal-Ruun). After the disappearance of central Imperial authority, ~200 independent entities entered into a struggle for control over:

| Resource | Significance | Controlling Clans |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Arable land (Thar Valley) | Food | Thar-Vex, Khar-Ur, Shan-En |
| Khar-Tor mines | Metals | Vexan, Khar-Tor, Moraat |
| Serrin Coast | Trade, salt | Serrin-Tash, Thalas-Vek |
| Temple-libraries | Knowledge (control weapon) | Keepers (neutral) |

**Types of conflicts:**
1. **Resource wars** (−2,100 – −2,050) — direct clashes over land and water
2. **Raiding** (−2,050 – −2,000) — mutual exhaustion, transition to raids
3. **City sieges** (−2,000 – −1,950) — destruction of fortified settlements

**Consequences of the Clan War:**
- ~60% of Thar Valley irrigation systems destroyed
- Technologies for large-scale construction lost
- Cultural ties between continents severed (Rhuval and Vexar isolated)
- Phenomenon of **"fortress-cities"** emerged — settlements surrounded by mud-brick walls
- Institution of **"neutral Keepers"** arose — clans that did not participate in the war and preserved knowledge in fortified libraries

> **KEY FACT**
>
> It was the Clan War that led to the formation of Xha'Ruun's unique ethics: the trauma of centuries of fratricidal conflict ingrained in culture the highest value of **unity (rass)** and **knowledge (ven)** as the only forces capable of uniting fragmented clans. The Council of Unity millennia later would be built on principles born precisely in this era.

#### 7.5.2.2 Era of "Fortress-Cities" (−1,950 – −1,800)

After the exhaustion of the Clan War came the period of **"fortress-cities"** — every major city became an autonomous fortified entity controlling the surrounding lands.

| Fortress-City | Walls | Population | Feature |
|---------------|-------|------------|---------|
| Khar-Vexar (Old City) | 10 sha high, 4.9 sha wide | 12,000 | Survived thanks to the temple |
| Shan-En | Natural cliff | 8,000 | Impregnable citadel |
| Ven-Khal (settlement) | Wood-earth rampart | 4,000 | Refuge for scholars |
| Thar-Vex (New) | 3.7 sha high | 2,000 | Rebuilt on ruins |

Key innovation of this era — **"Knowledge for Protection" system**: Library Keepers provided shelter to scientists, artisans, and physicians in exchange for copying books and teaching apprentices. Thanks to this system, ~15% of the First Empire's written heritage was preserved.

### 7.5.3 Industrial Revival (−1,800 – −750)

Gradually, through centuries of fragmentation, recovery began:

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| −1,500 | Invention of the water mill | Mechanization of labor |
| −1,350 | Resumption of intercontinental navigation | Trade |
| **−1,200** | **Steam engine** (Thal-Ven) | **Beginning of industrialization** |
| −1,050 | First railroad | Transport revolution |
| −900 | Telegraph (optical) | High-speed communication |
| −800 | Resumption of telescope building | Astronomy |
| **−750** | **Signing of the "Unity Treaty"** | **End of Crisis** |

**−1,200 y.c.** — engineer **Thal-Ven** from the independent city-state Shan-En (Kharak) demonstrates the first working steam engine. This date is considered the beginning of the industrial era and the technological watershed between antiquity and modernity.

## 7.6 Renaissance Era (−750 – +850)

### 7.6.1 Unity Treaty

**−750 y.c.** — signing of the "Unity Treaty" (Ven-Rass-Dok) between the 8 largest regions. The treaty:
1. Established common laws of trade and diplomacy

## 7.7 Era of Unity (+850 – present)

### 7.7.1 Foundation of the Council of Unity

**+850 y.c.** — in Khar-Vexar, the **Council of Unity** (Rass-Vokh-Sha) is convened — the supreme representative body of 49 members, elected by guilds and regions. The first chair elected is **Ven-Thal-Vek** — physicist and diplomat.

**Structure of the Council of Unity:**
- 49 members
- Term of office: 10 years
- Elections: every 2 years, 1/5 of the composition is renewed
- Guilds delegate 24 members, regions — 25
- No one may be elected more than 2 times

### 7.7.2 Technological Progress of the Era of Unity

| Date | Achievement | Significance |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| +1,200 | First landing on Thel | Second moon |
| +1,420 | **Controlled thermonuclear fusion** | Practically inexhaustible energy |
| +1,800 | Quantum computer | Bio-optical hybrid |
| +2,100 | First orbital elevator (equator) | Space cargo flow |
| +2,200 | Base on Xhel (aerostat) | Research station |
| +2,800 | Drilling station on Veksarr | Industrial mining |
| +3,500 | Probe to heliosphere boundary | Edge of the system |
| **+3,800** | **Launch of interstellar probe** | To the nearest star |

### 7.7.3 Social Development

The Era of Unity is characterized by:
- Stable population growth (from 1.2 billion to 2.8 billion)
- Eradication of hunger (fusion energy + automation)
- High level of education (universal literacy)
- Cultural flourishing (literature, music, architecture)

| −750 – +850 | Paper | "History of the World" | Good |
| +850 – present | Digital archives | Unified Knowledge Registry | Complete |

### 7.10.2 Problem of Reconstruction

Historians of the Era of Unity face the typical problem of ancient history: the Dark Ages periods (−2,100 – −1,800) are poorly documented. Exact dates before −5,000 are known with a margin of error of ±20–50 years. Chronology before −9,000 has a margin of error of ±100–200 years.
