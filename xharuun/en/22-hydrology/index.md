## Introduction

Fresh water makes up only 2.8% of Théxar's entire hydrosphere, but it is the foundation of terrestrial life. Rivers, lakes, groundwater, and glaciers form a single hydrological network connecting the oceans with the continents.

### 22.1.2 Khar-Tor River

The largest river on the planet. It originates from the glaciers of the Khar-Tor range at an altitude of 7,560 ша and empties into the Serrin Sea. Its basin covers ~24% of the Kharak continent.

**Hydrological regime:**
- Recharge: glacial (45%) + rain (40%) + groundwater (15%)
- High water: month Tor-Ven, discharge up to 69,000 ша³/s
- Low water: month Mor-Khal, discharge 11,300 ша³/s
- Solid runoff: ~194 billion кх/year (anomalously high due to volcanic ash)

**Economic significance:**
- Irrigation of 49.6 thous. мо² of agricultural land
- HEP cascade (6 stations, total capacity 48 power units)
- Navigable for 427 мо from the mouth

### 22.1.3 Thar River

### 22.2.2 Lake Mor-Ven

The largest lake on the planet, located on the Central Plain of Kharak. The name means "time-water."

**Characteristics:**
- Area: 1,768 мо²
- Average depth: 390 ша
- Maximum: 756 ша
- Volume: 115 мо³
- Transparency: 14.6 ша (Secchi)
- pH: 7.4

Lake Mor-Ven is a center of freshwater species endemism: 45% of fish species are found nowhere else.
