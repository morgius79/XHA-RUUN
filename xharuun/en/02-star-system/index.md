## Introduction

A star system is a gravitationally bound configuration in which one star (or several) holds around itself many smaller bodies: planets, asteroids, comets, dust. The Khar'Vex system — home of the Xha'Ruun civilization — is a well-ordered architecture of eight planets, an asteroid belt, and an extensive outer region of icy bodies.

Understanding the structure of the star system is necessary to answer key questions: why Thexar became habitable, what resources are available to the civilization, what cosmic threats it faces, and where it can expand.

## 2.1 The Star Khar'Vex: Detailed Profile

### 2.1.1 General Parameters

> **PROFILE: Star Khar'Vex**
>
> | Parameter | Value |
> |-----------|-------|
> | Spectral class | **K2V** |
> | Mass | 0.82 stellar mass |
> | Radius | 0.78 stellar radius |
> | Luminosity | 0.41 stellar luminosity |
> | Photosphere temperature | 2,643°X |
> | Age | 6.5 billion cycles |
> | Metallicity [Fe/H] | +0.12 |
> | Rotation period | 28.5 days |
> | Axial tilt | 7.3° |
> | X-ray luminosity | 3.1 × 10²⁷ erg/mig |
> | Activity (log R'HK index) | −5.02 |

**Spectral classification:** K2V — orange dwarf of the main sequence. The second index "V" indicates that the star is on the main sequence, stably burning hydrogen in its core. The subclass "K2" means the photosphere temperature (~2,643°X) places it in the lower part of the K-star range.

> **KEY FACT**
>
> Orange dwarfs (spectral class K) are considered "optimal" stars for the emergence of complex life. They have a main-sequence lifespan of up to 20–30 billion cycles (2–3× longer than G-class stars), giving evolution an order of magnitude more time. At the same time, they are bright enough to support photosynthesis and calm enough not to sterilize planets with flares.

### 2.1.2 Internal Structure

Like other K-class stars, Khar'Vex has a radiative core and a convective outer envelope. The boundary between these zones (the tachocline) is located deeper than in G-class stars — at ~0.65 radius, which explains the lower magnetic activity.

### 2.1.3 Spectrum and Color

The spectrum of Khar'Vex is shifted toward the red region. The peak emission occurs at ~570 nm (yellow-orange).

This spectral shift had a fundamental impact on the evolution of life on Thexar:
- Xha'Ruun photosynthetic pigments evolved to absorb in the orange-red region — peak absorption ~570–620 nm
- Xha'Ruun vision includes tetrachromatic coverage with peak sensitivity in the yellow-orange range
- The relative deficit of UV radiation allowed life to emerge on land — with a lower risk of mutagenic damage (UV radiation is ~1.5% of total, versus ~5% for G-class stars)
- The sky of Thexar has a characteristic **golden-bronze** hue (see [Ch.03, "Atmosphere"])

### 2.1.4 Magnetic Activity and Cycles

Khar'Vex exhibits a magnetic activity cycle with a smaller amplitude than G-class stars. The number of spots at cycle maximum is ~30–40% of that typical for G-dwarfs.

| Parameter | Khar'Vex |
|-----------|----------|
| Activity cycle | 9.1 years |
| Spots at maximum | 30–40% |
| Flares (X-class) | 1–2 per cycle |
| Coronal mass ejections | Rare, weak |
| Luminosity variation | ±0.08% |

> **KEY FACT**
>
> The calm nature of Khar'Vex is one of the reasons Thexar was able to develop complex life. If the star were more active (like young K-dwarfs), regular flares could have sterilized the planet's surface. The "window of calm" opened ~3 billion cycles ago when the star stabilized.

### 2.1.5 Future of Khar'Vex

As a star with mass 0.82, Khar'Vex will spend ~17 billion years on the main sequence (time measured in cycles from the present). The current age (6.5 billion cycles) means the star has passed approximately **one third** of its life path.

| Stage | Time rel. to present | What happens |
|-------|---------------------|--------------|
| +0 | Now | Stable hydrogen burning |
| +~6 billion years | Hydrogen in core runs out | Star begins to expand |
| +~7 billion years | Subgiant | Luminosity increases 2–3× |
| +~8 billion years | Red giant (RGB phase) | Radius increases tens of times, luminosity hundreds |
| +~8.5 billion years | Helium ignition (flash) | Brief stabilization |
| +~9 billion years | Horizontal branch | Helium burning in core |
| +~9.3 billion years | Asymptotic giant branch | Radius reaches Thexar's orbit |
| +~9.5 billion years | Planetary nebula | Shedding of outer layers |
| +~9.6 billion years | White dwarf | Cooling over trillions of years |

**Practical consequence:** the Xha'Ruun civilization has at least **1.2–1.5 billion cycles** to leave Thexar before Khar'Vex's increasing luminosity makes it uninhabitable.

### 2.2.4 Habitable Zone

The habitable zone is the range of orbits where temperature allows liquid water to exist on the planet's surface with sufficient atmospheric pressure.

| Parameter | Value | Note |
|-----------|-------|------|
| Inner boundary (conservative) | 0.44 AU | Ocean evaporation |
| Outer boundary (conservative) | 0.82 AU | Maximum greenhouse effect |
| Inner boundary (optimistic) | 0.38 AU | For thin atmospheres |
| Outer boundary (optimistic) | 0.95 AU | With dense CO₂ atmosphere |
| Position of Thexar | 0.63 AU | **In center of habitable zone** |

Thanks to its location in the **center of the habitable zone**, Thexar has a greater safety margin — a ~10% increase in Khar'Vex's luminosity over the next billion cycles will make the planet hot, but will not completely destroy life.

## 2.3 Inner Planets

### 2.3.1 Vax: Scorched World

> **PROFILE: Vax**
>
> | Parameter | Value |
> |-----------|-------|
> | Semi-major axis | 0.21 AU |
> | Mass | 0.43 (rel. Thexar) |
> | Radius | 0.63 (rel. Thexar) |
> | g | 13.3 sha/mig² |
> | Surface temperature | +273°X (day) / +143°X (night) |
> | Atmosphere | None |
> | Rotation period | 33.6 days (tidal lock) |
> | Tectonics | Inactive |

Vax is the closest planet to the star, having undergone tidal locking. One side is constantly facing Khar'Vex (scorching, +273°X), the other in eternal darkness (+143°X — still hot due to a powerful greenhouse layer of residual atmosphere).

**Interest for civilization:** Vax has no resources accessible for mining. However, its gravitational field is used for gravity assist maneuvers of probes heading to the inner regions of the system.

### 2.3.2 Xhel: Greenhouse Trap

> **PROFILE: Xhel**
>
> | Parameter | Value |
> |-----------|-------|
> | Semi-major axis | 0.42 AU |
> | Mass | 0.92 (rel. Thexar) |
> | Radius | 0.95 (rel. Thexar) |
> | g | 12.3 sha/mig² |
> | Atmosphere | CO₂ (96%), N₂ (3%), SO₂ (0.5%), traces |
> | Surface pressure | **455 tyazh** |
> | Surface temperature | **+296°X** |
> | Rotation period | 92.8 days (tidal lock) |

Xhel is a classic example of a **runaway greenhouse effect**. Being too close to the star, the planet lost its oceans. The remaining CO₂ atmosphere created monstrous pressure, heating the surface to temperatures exceeding the melting point of lead.

> **RELATION**
>
> Xhel serves the Xha'Ruun as a **warning and a laboratory**. By studying this planet, Thexar's climatologists better understand the boundaries of habitability. Additionally, the upper layers of Xhel's atmosphere (at an altitude of 73–97 km, where temperature ≈ 45°X) are used as a base for research stations — see [Ch.09, "Planetary Bases"].

Despite the hellish conditions, Xhel has scientific value:
- The dense atmosphere allows aerodynamic braking of probes
- The upper atmospheric layers contain isotopes of helium and deuterium
- Geologically active (volcanism due to tidal heating)

## 2.4 Asteroid Belt

Between the orbits of Thexar and Zhal, at a distance of 1.15–1.65 AU from Khar'Vex, lies the asteroid belt — a remnant of the protoplanetary disk that failed to form a full planet due to the gravitational influence of Rhuun.

> **PROFILE: Asteroid Belt**
>
> | Parameter | Value |
> |-----------|-------|
> | Distance from star | 1.15–1.65 AU |
> | Total mass | 0.03 (rel. Thexar) |
> | Number of objects >10 km (diam.) | ~35,000 |
> | Number of objects >100 km (diam.) | ~120 |
> | Largest object | **Veksarr** (diameter 412 km) |
> | Composition | Rocky (80%), metallic (15%), carbonaceous (5%) |

**Veksarr** — the largest asteroid in the belt, having its own gravity and classified as a dwarf planet. Its core is rich in nickel-iron alloys and platinum group metals.

> **KEY FACT**
>
> The asteroid belt is the **main mineral resource** of the Xha'Ruun civilization. Metal mining (Fe, Ni, Pt, Au, Rh) is carried out on ~40 of the largest asteroids. A single 1 km diameter asteroid can contain more platinum than was mined in all of Thexar's history.

Rhuun is a gas giant, the dominant gravitational force of the outer system. Its mass constitutes ~20% of the mass of all planets and asteroids in the system combined (excluding the star itself).

**Rhuun's ring system** is a majestic sight, visible from Thexar through a telescope as a bright ring ~24,400 mo wide, consisting of icy and rocky particles ranging from dust grains to boulders of 12 sha.

> **KEY FACT**
>
> Rhuun's largest moon — **Thalass** — is the **primary candidate for off-planet colonization**. It possesses:
> - A thin nitrogen-methane atmosphere (0.08 atm — ≈0.08 tyazh)
> - A subsurface ocean of liquid water (beneath an ice crust ~24,400 sha thick)
> - Geological activity (tidal heating from Rhuun)
> - Presence of complex organic compounds

Koss is an ice giant. Its characteristic pale-blue color is explained by methane haze in the upper atmospheric layers. Axial tilt: 82° — the planet "lies on its side," creating extreme seasonal effects at the poles.

### 2.5.4 Phor: Outskirts Sentinel

Phor is a dwarf planet on the far outskirts of the system, in a highly eccentric orbit (0.21). Its orbit stretches from 14.7 to 22.5 AU from the star, crossing the outer regions of the system along an elliptical trajectory.

Phor consists predominantly of water and methane ice, with a small rocky core. Its only moon is a tiny icy fragment ~30 km in diameter.

> **TERM: Khar Cloud**
>
> The outer region of the Khar'Vex system, extending from ~30 to ~800 AU from the star. Contains ~10¹²–10¹³ cometary nuclei — icy bodies ranging from 0.1 to 20 km in size. It is the source of long-period comets that occasionally visit the inner system.

## 2.6 Heliosphere and Space Weather

### 2.6.1 Stellar Wind

Khar'Vex emits a stream of charged particles (stellar wind) with an average speed of ~775 mo/mig and a density of ~2 particles/cm³ at Thexar's orbit.

| Parameter | Khar'Vex (at 0.63 AU) |
|-----------|-----------------------|
| Wind speed | ~775 mo/mig |
| Density | ~2 cm⁻³ |
| Magnetic field strength | ~3 nT |
| Temperature | ~4.4 × 10⁴ °X |

### 2.6.3 Radiation Environment on Thexar

Thanks to the combination of:
1. **Khar'Vex's calm nature** (rare flares)
2. **Thexar's magnetosphere**
3. **Dense atmosphere**

— the radiation background on Thexar's surface is ~0.8 mSv/year, which is natural for the planet.

> **RELATION**
>
> The low radiation background is one of the reasons Xha'Ruun live longer (see [Ch.05, "Physiology"]), but also a reason they are more vulnerable to cosmic radiation outside the magnetosphere — an important factor when planning space missions (see [Ch.09, "Space Technologies"]).

## 2.7 Comets and Small Bodies

### 2.7.1 Comets

Comets are icy bodies on elongated orbits, delivering volatiles (water, CO₂, CH₄, NH₃) from the outer system to the inner system. For Thexar, comets have a dual significance:

| Aspect | Positive | Negative |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Water | Delivery of H₂O to surface | — |
| Organics | Import of prebiotic molecules | — |
| Collisions | — | Destruction, mass extinctions |

**Comet defense:** The Xha'Ruun have created a system for early detection and interception of potentially hazardous objects (see [Ch.09, "Planetary Defense"]).

### 2.7.2 Meteorite Activity

Average annual number of meteorite falls on Thexar ~7,300 ru per year.
