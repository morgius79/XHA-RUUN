## 27.1 Geodetic System

### 27.1.1 Reference Ellipsoid

The Théxar coordinate system is based on the **WGS-Théxar** reference ellipsoid, approved by the Guild of Navigators in +2 200 E.U.

| Parameter | Value |
|----------|----------|
| Equatorial radius (a) | 1 242 mo |
| Polar radius (b) | 1 238 mo |
| Flattening (f) | 1/298.3 |
| Mean radius (R₀) | 1 241 mo |
| Equatorial circumference | 7 805 mo |
| Meridian circumference | 7 781 mo |
| Surface area (ellipsoid) | 19.4 million mo² |
| Volume | 8.03 × 10⁹ mo³ |

### 27.1.2 Geoid

The Théxar geoid is a model of the planet's shape accounting for gravitational anomalies. Geoid deviations from WGS-Théxar:

| Region | Anomaly (sha) | Cause |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Khar-Tor Ridge | +51 sha | Gravitational excess (mountain mass) |
| Thash Trench | −46 sha | Gravitational deficit (deep-sea trench) |
| Raal Plateau | +22 sha | Excess crustal density |
| Shan Desert | −15 sha | Thin crust |

## 27.2 Coordinate System

### 27.2.1 Geographic Coordinates

The Xha'Ruun use a spherical coordinate system:

- **Latitude (φ):** 0° (equator) → +90° (north) / −90° (south)
- **Longitude (λ):** 0° (prime meridian) → +180° (east) / −180° (west)
- **Height (h):** above the WGS-Théxar reference ellipsoid

### 27.2.2 Prime Meridian

The prime meridian passes through the **center of the Government Quarter square of Khar-Vexar**, at coordinates:

- WGS-Théxar: φ = 42°18'24" N, λ = 0°00'00"
- Mark: bronze plate 1.2 sha in diameter at the center of the square

### 27.2.3 Equator

The Théxar equator crosses the southern tip of the Kharak continent and the northern tip of Rhuval. At the intersection with the 48° E meridian stands the **Equator Monument** — a 15-sha stele of black basalt.

### 27.5.2 Satellite Geodesy

24 GNSS (Global Navigation Satellite System) satellites in orbits at altitudes of 3 660–4 470 mo provide:

- Positioning accuracy: ±2.3 pa (civilian), ±0.58 pa (military)
- Coverage: 100% of Théxar's surface
- Fix time: <10 s
