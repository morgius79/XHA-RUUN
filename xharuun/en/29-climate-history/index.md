## 29.2 Climatic Cycles

### 29.2.1 Orbital Cycles (Kharak Cycles)

| Cycle | Period (cycles) | T Amplitude | Cause |
|------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Orbital eccentricity | 95 thousand | ±0.7°X | Change in orbit shape |
| Axial tilt | 41 thousand | ±0.4°X | Nutation |
| Precession | **38.6 thousand** | ±0.3°X | Equinox drift |

Théxar's precession period is 38.6 thousand cycles and is determined by the tidal influence of the moons Serrin and Thel.

| After Tor-Ven eruption | 9 200 | +0.7°X | Volcanic emission |

### 29.4.2 Equilibrium Greenhouse Effect

Théxar is in a state of equilibrium greenhouse effect: CO₂ = 0.8% (8 000 ppm) compensates for low insolation. Regulation mechanism — silicate weathering and volcanic degassing. Equilibrium establishment time: ~500 thousand cycles.

## 29.5 Modern Changes

Over the last 500 cycles, Théxar's average temperature has risen by +0.2°X.

| Period | T Anomaly | Cause |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| 3 520–3 700 | −0.2°X | Mini-ice age phase |
| 3 700–3 900 | +0.05°X | Exit from mini-ice age |
| 3 900–4 028 | +0.2°X | Natural variation |

Current warming is not linked to anthropogenic impact — Xha'Ruun civilization CO₂ emissions amount to <0.1% of the natural flux (fusion energy).

## Chapter Summary

1. Théxar's climate is governed by orbital cycles: 95 thousand, 41 thousand, 38.6 thousand cycles.
2. The last glaciation reached its maximum 32 thousand cycles ago.
3. Precession (38.6 thousand cycles) — the shortest due to the influence of the moons.
4. Over the last 500 cycles: warming of +0.2°X (natural).
5. Equilibrium greenhouse effect maintains CO₂ at 8 000 ppm.
6. Ice cores: continuous record over 500 thousand cycles.
