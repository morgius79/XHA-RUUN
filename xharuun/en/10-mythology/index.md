## Introduction

The religion of Xha'Ruun — **"Teaching of Vibration"** (Sha'Thar-Ven — "language of truth-power") — is a unique synthesis of polytheism, pantheism, and scientific cosmology. Unlike many known religious systems, the Teaching of Vibration does not contradict the scientific worldview but complements it: physical laws are seen as the "grammar" in which the gods "wrote" the universe.

The orthodox Teaching is professed by ~78% of Thexar's population. Atheism (~12%) and small cults (~10%) are tolerated, though perceived as "incomplete understanding."

## 10.1 Cosmogony: Creation Myth

### 10.1.1 Primordial Vibration

According to the orthodox Teaching, before the beginning there was neither space, nor time, nor matter — there was only the **One** (Vokh-Ven), a state of absolute symmetry and stillness. Then, for an unknown reason, a **fluctuation** arose within the One — the Primordial Vibration (*Khar-Vokh*, lit. "Light-Vibration").

> **KEY FACT**
>
> The term "Primordial Vibration" in a scientific context is identified with quantum fluctuations at the moment of the Big Bang. For Xha'Ruun, this is not a metaphor but a direct reference: religion and cosmology describe the same event in different languages. "The language of physics is the language in which the gods speak to scientists" (see → [Ch.01, "Cosmology of Culture"]).

The Primordial Vibration gave rise to the **Seven Voices** — primordial entities, each of which contributed to the creation of the universe.

### 10.1.3 Cycles of Creation

The cosmos, according to the Teaching, exists in cycles called **"Great Breath"** (Mor-Vokh):

1. **Exhalation** — expansion of the universe (current phase)
2. **Pause** — maximum expansion
3. **Inhalation** — contraction of the universe
4. **Silence** — singularity, then a new Exhalation

This directly corresponds to the cyclic universe cosmological model discussed by Thexar physicists (→ see [Ch.01, "Future of the Universe"]).

### 10.2.2 Theodicy: The Problem of Evil

The Teaching of Vibration resolves the problem of the existence of evil (suffering, catastrophes, death) through the concept of **necessary dissonance**:

> **KEY FACT**
>
> In the Teaching of Vibration, **Ghal-Thar** (battle-strength) is not an opponent of the other Voices. He is a necessary part of the symphony. Without entropy there would be no time. Without conflict — no development. Without death — no renewal. Evil and suffering are not an error in creation, but a necessary condition for the existence of free will and evolution. "A symphony without dissonance is not a symphony, but the hum of a single note."

### 10.4.2 Musical Meditations

The main form of religious practice is **collective singing** (Sha-Rass). Worshippers gather in the temple and sing multi-frequency chorales. Thanks to two pairs of vocal cords (→ see [Ch.05, "Physiology"]), priests can produce two tones simultaneously.

**Structure of a typical meditation:**
1. **Tuning** (5–10 min) — individual humming at the temple's resonant frequency
2. **Ascension** (15–30 min) — gradual harmonic complexity, addition of overtones
3. **Climax** (5–15 min) — full chorale, all voices merge
4. **Dissolution** (5–10 min) — gradual fading, return to silence

## 10.5 Eschatology

### 10.5.1 Individual Eschatology

After death, according to the Teaching, the consciousness of Xha'Ruun returns to the **Great Silence** (Vokh-Zhal) — a state similar to the Primordial Vibration. Then, after a period of "intermission" (duration depending on lifetime merits), consciousness can **reincarnate** into a new life form.

Levels of reincarnation (in ascending order):
1. **Unicellular** — minimal consciousness
2. **Plants** — environmental perception
3. **Lower animals** — instincts
4. **Higher animals** — emotions, learning
5. **Xha'Ruun** — sapience

The goal of spirit evolution is the successive ascent through these levels until reaching the **Seventh Level** — complete merging with the One (liberation from the cycle of reincarnation).

### 10.5.2 Universal Eschatology

At the end of the current cycle (the "Great Breath"), the universe will cease expanding and begin to contract. At the moment of the Great Contraction, all Voices will merge into a single note, which will return to the Silence. After Silence will follow a new "Inspiration" — the birth of a new universe.

> **RELATION**
>
> The cyclic eschatology of the Teaching of Vibration remarkably aligns with the cyclic cosmological models considered by Thexar physicists (→ see [Ch.01, "Future of the Universe"]). The difference is only that religion asserts: the new cycle will be **meaningful** — the universe is born not randomly, but as an act of creation flowing from the nature of the Primordial Vibration.

## 10.6 Religion and Science

### 10.6.1 Synthesis

One of the unique features of Xha'Ruun civilization is the **absence of conflict between religion and science**. Xha'Ruun see them as **complementary languages describing one reality**.

| Language | Object | Term for "beginning" | Term for "law" |
|----------|--------|---------------------|-----------------|
| **Religion** | Meaning, purpose | Primordial Vibration | Will of the Voices |
| **Science** | Mechanism, cause | Quantum fluctuation | Fundamental constants |
| **Synthesis** | Physics says "how," religion says "why" | One process | One reality |

### 10.6.2 Sources of Synthesis

This attitude has historical roots:
1. **Flourishing Era** (−6,200 – −2,100): science and religion developed together, priests were scientists
2. **Era of Crisis** (−2,100 – −750): joint survival strengthened the alliance
3. **Era of Unity** (+850 – present): the guild of Scholars and the guild of Keepers sit in the Council of Unity as equals

### 10.6.3 Atheism

Atheism (~12% of the population) is tolerated. Atheists generally consider the Vibration a physical process without conscious intention. However, even atheists participate in musical meditations (as a cultural practice) and observe ethical norms based on the Teaching.
