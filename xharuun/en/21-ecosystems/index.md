## Introduction

Théxar's ecosystems are the product of 4 billion cycles of evolution under an orange star, active tectonics, and a sulfur-rich biosphere. The fundamental difference of Théxar's ecosystems is the presence of a **sulfur cycle** (H₂S), parallel to the carbon cycle and playing a dominant role in a number of biomes.

Théxar has formed 10 major terrestrial biomes, 5 marine, and 3 unique ecosystems (hydrothermal vents, cave systems, subglacial lakes) without direct analogs.

### 21.1.2 Sulfur Cycle

The sulfur cycle is the key difference of Théxar's ecosystems; on Théxar, sulfur is a **full-fledged analog of nitrogen** in biogeochemical cycles.

`
                    THÉXAR SULFUR CYCLE

        Atmosphere                   H₂S (0.0008%)
           ▲                             │
           │  Volcanic                   │  Biogenic
           │  emissions                  │  emission
           │                             ▼
        ┌──┴─────────────────────────────────┐
        │  Biosphere: H₂S → organic S       │
        │  Chemosynthesis: H₂S + CO₂ → org. │
        │  Photosynthesis: S-amino acids     │
        └──┬─────────────────────────────────┘
           │                             ▲
           │  Mineralization             │  Assimilation
           ▼                             │
        ┌──┴─────────────────────────────────┐
        │  Soils/Ocean: sulfates (SO₄²⁻)   │
        │  FeS₂ (pyrite), H₂S (dissolved)  │
        └───────────────────────────────────┘
           │                             ▲
           │  Leaching                   │  Volcanic
           ▼                             │  degassing
        ┌──┴─────────────────────────────────┐
        │  Lithosphere: FeS₂, CaSO₄, S°    │
        │  Mantle sulfur                   │
        └────────────────────────────────────┘
`

## 21.2 Trophic Levels

### 21.2.1 Producers

Primary production of Théxar's ecosystems is estimated at ~120 Gt C/year. The reason for high productivity is more efficient photosynthesis (4.2%).

| Producer group | Contribution | Mechanism |
|----------------|-------------|-----------|
| Terrestrial plants (forests) | 58% | Chromophyll photosynthesis |
| Phytoplankton (oceans) | 28% | Chromophyll + phycobilin |
| Chemoautotrophs (H₂S) | 8% | Chemosynthesis (hydrothermal, soils) |
| Algae (freshwater) | 4% | Chromophyll |
| Other (cryptoendoliths) | 2% | Chemosynthesis + photosynthesis |

### 21.2.2 Food Chains

**Grazing chain (Kharak savannas):**

`
  Chromophyta (grasses)
       ↓
  Ghal-Shar (herbivore, 2nd level)    → Rhu-Hopper (scavenger)
       ↓
  Shar-Ghar (predator, 3rd level)
       ↓
  (rarely) Tor-Mor (superpredator, 4th level)
`

**Detrital chain (Vexar forests):**

`
  Leaf litter (from Ven-Shar)
       ↓
  Khal-Burrower (detritivore) + Mycophyta fungi
       ↓
  Rhu-Hopper (scavenger) → Vex-Thorn (predator)
`

## 21.3 Unique Ecosystems

### 21.3.1 Equatorial Jungles

Théxar's jungles are the most productive terrestrial biome. Located in the equatorial belt (0°–8° latitude) in southern Kharak and northern Rhuval.

Structure:

| Layer | Height | Closure | Species | Photosynthesis |
|-------|--------|---------|---------|----------------|
| A (emergent) | 60–85 m | <10% | 120 | Full light |
| B (main canopy) | 30–60 m | 85% | 2,800 | Partial shade |
| C (understory) | 5–30 m | 60% | 4,200 | Heavy shade |
| D (shrubs) | 1–5 m | 30% | 3,800 | Low light |
| E (forest floor) | 0–1 m | <5% | 1,200 | Minimal |

Characteristic adaptation — **H₂S air-filtration**: leaves of lower layers have stomata capable of absorbing H₂S directly from the air and using it in metabolism (compensation for low light).

### 21.3.2 Cave Ecosystems

Théxar's karst systems (especially on the Raal plateau, Rhuval) form the largest underground ecosystems on the planet. Total explored cave length — ~69,100 мо.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Total area of underground ecosystems | ~33,000 мо² |
| Endemism | ~95% of species are endemics |
| Main energy source | H₂S + Fe²⁺ chemosynthesis |
| Average T | +51.9°X (stable ±0.3°X) |
| Largest cave | **Thal-Ven** (depth 3,460 ша, volume 871 million ша³) |
| Deepest | **Khar-Deep** (3,810 ша from surface) |

Cave ecosystems have been isolated from the surface for millions of cycles. Some species show complete loss of vision and pigmentation, replaced by developed electroreception and chemosensitivity.

### 21.3.3 Hydrothermal Oases

At least 12 hydrothermal vent fields have been discovered on the floor of Théxar's oceans. The largest:

| Field | Ocean | Depth | Vent T | Unique Feature |
|-------|-------|-------|--------|----------------|
| Tor-1 | Thalas | 3,900 ша | 232.7°X | Largest (0.33 мо²) |
| Thash-Deep | Gharra | 10,000 ша | 254.4°X | Deepest field on the planet |
| Gharra-Vent | Gharra | 5,850 ша | 213.6°X | Rich in sulfur and REE |

These ecosystems are living fossils, preserving the metabolic pathways of ancient Théxar.

### 21.3.4 Mangrove Forests

Théxar's mangrove ecosystems are distributed along the coasts of all three continents. Their key difference is **silicon biomineralization** of roots, allowing trees to grow in water with a high concentration of dissolved H₂S (up to 2.5× lethal for ordinary plants).

| Region | Area (мо²) | Dominant | Productivity |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------------|
| Southern coast of Kharak | 19,800 | Mangro-Ven | 12 t C/ha/yr |
| Thar Delta | 5,000 | Mangro-Khal | 9 t C/ha/yr |
| Rhuval coast | 13,200 | Mangro-Thal | 10 t C/ha/yr |
| Vexar bays | 11,600 | Mangro-Vex | 8 t C/ha/yr |

## 21.4 Seasonal Dynamics

### 21.4.1 Seasons on Théxar

Due to the small axial tilt (14.2°), seasonal changes are less pronounced:

| Zone | Winter T | Summer T | Amplitude | Precipitation (seasonality) |
|------|---------|---------|-----------|-----------------------------|
| Equatorial | +60.3°X | +61.4°X | ~1.1°X | Uniform |
| Tropical | +54.9°X | +60.3°X | ~5.4°X | Dry/wet |
| Temperate | +47.3°X | +53.8°X | ~6.5°X | Moderate seasonality |
| Subpolar | +40.7°X | +47.3°X | ~6.5°X | Snow summer/winter |
| Polar | +31.5°X | +42.4°X | ~10.9°X | Minimum precipitation |

### 21.4.2 Migrations

The Great Migration of **Ghal-Shar** — the largest seasonal animal movement on the planet. Twice a year, herds totaling 2–4 million individuals cross the Central Plain of Kharak, following the seasonal rains.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Population | 2–4 million individuals |
| Distance | up to 305 мо |
| Duration | 4–6 weeks |
| Speed | 5.1–7.1 мо/day |
| Mortality | 8–12% (predators, disease, old age) |

## Chapter Summary

**Key conclusions:**
1. Théxar has 10 terrestrial and 5 marine biomes with a total primary production of ~120 Gt C/year.
2. The sulfur cycle is the key difference of Théxar's ecosystems; H₂S participates in metabolism on par with carbon.
3. Endemism is highest in deserts (42%), mangroves (45%), and inland waters (52%).
4. Cave ecosystems — the largest underground biomes on the planet (33 thous. мо², 95% endemism).
5. Equatorial jungles are five-layered, with H₂S air-filtration in lower layers.
6. Great Migration of Ghal-Shar — up to 4 million individuals, 305 мо, twice a year.
