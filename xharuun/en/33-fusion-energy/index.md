## 33.1 Thorium Cycle

### 33.1.1 Principle

The thorium fuel cycle — a process in which ²³²Th (the only natural isotope of thorium) captures a neutron and transforms into ²³³U — fissile material:

²³²Th + n → ²³³Th (β⁻, 22.3 min) → ²³³Pa (β⁻, 27.0 days) → ²³³U (fission)

Advantage: burnup of ~98% of fuel (vs. ~1% in uranium reactors).

## 33.2 He-3 Cycle

### 33.2.1 Reaction

D + ³He → ⁴He (3.6 MeV) + p (14.7 MeV)

The D-³He reaction — the "holy grail" of fusion. Its advantages:

- Products: ⁴He + proton (not neutrons) — minimal radioactivity
- High energy yield: 18.3 MeV per reaction
- No reactor material activation
