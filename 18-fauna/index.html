Chapter 18: Fauna of Théxar

"The beasts of Théxar are our younger siblings. In each of them flows the same blood as in us, and the same stars shine upon them. The difference is only that we ask questions, while they are answers we have not yet learned to hear." — From the "Bestiary of Xha'Ruun," Academy of Natural History, +2,100 Y.E.

Related sections: → see [Ch.11, "Ecology"], → see [Ch.17, "Flora"], → see [Ch.05, "Species Xha'Ruun"] Volume: 40 pages Key principle: Théxar's fauna is based on the class Thermochromadae ("warm-colored") — hexapodal vertebrates with an exo-endoskeleton combination, cutaneous thermoregulation, and a double circulatory system. ~24,000 species, from 0.2 ша to 9.8 ша.

Introduction

The animal world of Théxar is one of the richest among known biospheres. Evolution under the orange sky of Khar'Vex, with elevated radiation background and a unique biochemical foundation, led to the formation of a fauna without direct analogs.

The fundamental difference of Théxar's fauna is hexapodal symmetry (six-limbedness). Evolution on Théxar took the path of six limbs. This is not accidental: the combination of lower gravity, denser atmosphere (1.00 тяж), and a different spectral composition of light created the preconditions for the development of a more complex locomotor system.

In this chapter, we examine the evolution, systematics, and ecology of the animal world of Théxar: from class Thermochromadae to invertebrates, from giant herbivores of the plains to microscopic zooplankton of the oceans.

18.1 Class Thermochromadae — Dominant Taxon

KEY FACT

The class Thermochromadae ("warm-colored") — an endemic vertebrate class of Théxar, to which Xha'Ruun also belong. Includes ~24,000 species — from small reptile-like (0.2 ша) to giant herbivores (9.8 ша). Common characteristics: hexapodaly, double circulatory system, cutaneous thermoregulation through chromatophores, egg-laying reproduction.

18.1.1 Key Characteristics of the Class

Trait Description Function
Hexapodaly 6 limbs (2 front, 2 middle, 2 hind) Locomotion + manipulation
Exo-endoskeleton Chitinous outer layer + bony internal skeleton Protection + support
Double circulation 2 hearts: thoracic + abdominal Separation of respiratory and systemic blood flow
Thermochromatism Cutaneous thermoregulation through pigment cells Heating at low T, cooling at high T
Trinocular vision 3 eyes (in higher forms) Depth perception, wide angle
Egg-laying Egg clutch with calcareous shell Reproduction
6-letter code Genetic code on 6 nucleotides Mutation resistance, protein complexity

18.2 Predators (Predatoria)

The order Predatoria — apex predators of Théxar, occupying niches from small forest hunters to large plains predators.

18.2.1 Large Predators

Species Size Mass Range Hunting Strategy
Shar-Ghar (Red Tiger) 4.3 ша × 2.2 ша 364–485 кх Plains of Kharak Ambush, pack (3–6 individuals)
Tor-Mor (Mountain Killer) 5.1 ша × 2.6 ша 545–727 кх Khar-Tor range Solitary, ambush
Rhu-Ven (Night Hunter) 3.4 ша × 1.7 ша 182–273 кх Forests of Rhuval Nocturnal, pack (8–15 individuals)
Vex-Thorn (Thorny Ambusher) 2.7 ша × 1.0 ша 73–109 кх Vexar Silicon spikes on tail

Shar-Ghar — the dominant predator of the Kharak plains. Coloration — copper-red with black stripes (camouflage in the savanna). Hunts in packs of 3–6 individuals, using trinocular vision for distance calculation. Average lifespan — 25 local years.

Tor-Mor — the largest terrestrial predator on the planet. Lives at altitudes of 0.3–0.9 мо in the Khar-Tor range. A solitary territorial predator. Capable of knocking down prey weighing up to 1.2 ру with a strike of its two front limbs.

18.3 Herbivores (Herbirostra)

Order Herbirostra — herbivorous Thermochromadae, from small to giant grazers.

18.3.1 Large Herbivores

Species Height Mass Range Status
Ghal-Shar (Plains Runner) 3.9 ша 1,091–1,515 кх Savannas of Kharak Herding (10–40)
Mor-Koss (Heavyhorn) 2.6 ша 1,818–2,727 кх Forests of Vexar Herding (5–15)

Height at shoulder: 4.6 ша. Total height with raised head — up to 7.9 ша.

Ghal-Shar — the most common large herbivore of the Kharak savannas. The front pair of limbs is used for browsing foliage, the middle pair for support and defense, the hind pair for running. Reaches speeds of up to 11.2 мо/h. Lifespan — up to 40 local years.

Khar-Moss — a large herbivore adapted to cold climate. Dense fur up to 0.6 ша long, subcutaneous fat up to 17.4 па. Migrates up to 304.9 мо between summer and winter pastures.

18.4 Kharru Group (Xa-ruunidae)

The family Xa-ruunidae (from xa — life, ruun — people) — the closest living relatives of Xha'Ruun among non-sapient species.

KEY FACT

Xa-ruunidae is the only family demonstrating rudimentary social intelligence: use of simple tools (stones for cracking nuts), transmission of cultural information (migration routes), complex sound communication (up to 40 distinguishable signals).

18.4.1 Main Species

18.4.2 Kharru — The Best Companion Animal

Kharru — a domesticated form, bred from a wild ancestor ~8,000 years ago. A six-legged animal 1.5 ша in size, mass 48–73 кх.

Characteristics:

  • Trinocular vision (lateral eyes — wide angle, central — depth)
  • Four ears (two front movable, two rear — directional hearing)
  • Chromophyll fur (golden-red, thick)
  • Omnivorous (predominantly herbivorous)
  • High trainability (commands up to 50 words)
  • Lifespan — 20–30 local years

Use in Xha'Ruun civilization:

  • Agriculture: herd protection, water finding
  • Transport: riding (up to 8.1 мо/h, 6 h per day)
  • Rescue work: search under rubble
  • Companion: kharru is a symbol of status and friendship

KEY FACT

Théxar's hexapods ("insects") descended from a separate branch of arthropods and have 4 wings. Wingspan of the largest species — 0.6 ша. Hexapod endemism by continent: up to 80% of species are unique to each continent.

18.6.3 Vexar — Fauna of Isolated Forests

Vexar, as the most isolated continent, has the highest level of fauna endemism — 38% (for plants — 31%).

Ecosystem Dominants Endemism
Broadleaf forests Vex-Thorn, Vex-Squeaker 42%
Mountain forests Tree-Jumper, Moss-Glide 52%
Mangrove coasts Shore-Wader, Mud-Crab 28%

Characteristic feature of Vexar's fauna — complete absence of large terrestrial predators (the ecological niche is filled by small pack species). This led to unique behavior in local herbivores: they are less skittish and more curious than their counterparts on other continents.

18.7 Seasonal Migrations

Théxar's fauna demonstrates impressive seasonal migrations driven by changing climate zones.

Species Distance Route Period Number
Ghal-Shar up to 304.9 мо Central Kharak → southern pastures Dry season 2–4 million
Shal-Mor (fish) up to 609.8 мо Thalas → Kharak spawning grounds Month Tor-Ven 500 million
Khar-Moss up to 304.9 мо Taiga → northern pastures Summer 200,000
Thal-Shark up to 813.0 мо Thalas → Gharra → Thalas 2-year cycle 50,000

The Great Migration of Ghal-Shar is one of the most spectacular events in Théxar's nature. Twice a year, herds of Ghal-Shar cross the Central Plain of Kharak, following the seasonal rains. The migration is followed by predators (Shar-Ghar) and scavengers (Rhu-Hopper).

Chapter Summary

Key conclusions:

  1. Class Thermochromadae — the dominant taxon of Théxar vertebrates (~24,000 species).
  2. Common characteristics: hexapodaly, double circulation, thermochromatism, egg-laying.
  3. Largest predator — Tor-Mor (545–727 кх, Khar-Tor range).
  4. Largest herbivore — Khar-Moss (up to 3,333 кх, taiga).
  5. Kharru — domesticated 8,000 years ago, closest living relative of Xha'Ruun.
  6. Fauna endemism of Vexar — 38% (52% in mountain forests).
  7. Hexapods ("insects") — ~1.5 million species, 4 wings, 80% endemics per continent.
  8. Deepest trench Thash (2.0 мо) contains a unique chemosynthesizing ecosystem.
  9. Great Migration of Ghal-Shar (304.9 мо) — the largest herbivore migration on the planet.
