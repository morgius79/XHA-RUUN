Volume II: History — Chapter 3: The First Empire (Era of Flourishing)

"Verksaren united the clans not with the sword — he united them with law. The Imperial Code — the first bond that turned a people into a civilization." — From "The Chronicles of the First Empire"

Related sections: → see [Vol. II, Ch.2], → see [Ch.07, "History"] Era: −6,200 – −2,100 (4,100 years) Volume: 30 pages

3.1 Founding

−6,150 c.y. — a key date in the history of Théxar. Verksaren (CH-0001), leader of the Thar Valley clan, unites the scattered city-states under a single authority. Over two decades of strategically calculated campaigns, he subdues the last independent enclaves and proclaims the First Xha'Ruun Empire.

Territory of the Empire at its founding: ~2.8 million km² (the entire continent of Kharak).

3.1.1 Verksan Dynasty

The Verksan dynasty ruled the First Empire for nine centuries:

Verkmiraen II −6,020 – −5,940 (80 yrs) Road construction
Verkmiraen III −5,940 – −5,880 (60 yrs) Mastery of metallurgy
Verkmiraen IV −5,880 – −5,820 (60 yrs) Contact with Rhuval
Verksaren II −5,820 – −5,780 (40 yrs) First expedition to Rhuval

3.1.2 The Imperial Code

The first code of laws, created by Verksaren I. 42 of 48 articles survive.

Principles of the Code:

  1. Equal for equal — restitution of harm, not vengeance
  2. Community responsibility for crimes committed by its member
  3. Inviolability of ambassadors and merchants
  4. Protection of the farmer from the arbitrary actions of tax collectors

3.2.2 Colonization of Rhuval

−5,600 — the first permanent Xha'ri settlements on the continent of Rhuval. By −4,000, Rhuval became an integral part of the Empire, though with some autonomy.

3.3 Social Structure

3.3.1 Imperial Hierarchy

Level Composition Share of Population
Emperor (Verksan) Supreme ruler <0.01%
Nobility Regional rulers, military commanders 2%
Master Guilds Craftsmen, merchants, scribes 15%
Free Farmers Main population 60%
Slaves Prisoners of war, debtors 23%

3.3.2 Slavery

Slavery existed but was limited by the Imperial Code: a slave had the right of ransom, inviolability of life, and their children were born free.

3.4 Cultural Flourishing

−4,500 – −3,000 — the cultural flourishing of the Empire:

  • Architecture: construction of palaces and temples from basalt
  • Literature: poems about the deeds of Verksaren
  • Science: astronomy, mathematics (sexagesimal system), medicine

The largest architectural monument — the Hall of the First (Council Hall in Verksar), residence of the Imperial Council.
