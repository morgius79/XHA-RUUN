Volume II: History — Chapter 2: Early Civilization

"First came the word. Not divine — rational. First came the mark on clay, a sign meaning 'grain'. Then — a sign meaning 'house'. Then — a sign meaning 'people'. Thus history was born." — From "The History of Writing", Academy of Khar-Vexar

Related sections: → see [Vol. II, Ch.1], → see [Ch.07, "History"] Era: −9,800 – −6,200 (3,600 years)

2.1 Invention of Writing

−9,780 c.y. — a key date in the history of civilization. The first proto-writing emerged in the Thar Valley.

Stage Period Type Example
Pictograms −9,800 – −9,500 Drawings ^^^ (grain)
Ideograms −9,500 – −9,200 Abstract signs ⨁ (god/spirit)
Syllabographs −9,200 – −8,500 Syllabic signs 𐤑𐰀 (xha)
Logograms −8,500 Word-signs 𐤑𐰀𐰺𐰆𐰣 (Xha'Ruun)
