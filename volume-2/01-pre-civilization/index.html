Volume II: History — Chapter 1: Pre-Civilization Era

"Before the first words were written, before the first walls were raised, before the first fire was tamed — we were already here. We looked at the stars and knew: we are not alone in the universe, but there are no others like us." — From the "Book of Origin", canonical text, Era of Unity

Introduction

The pre-civilization era of Théxar spans from the emergence of the first sapient Xha'ri (~15,000 years ago) to the invention of proto-writing (−9,780). It is an era for which no written records survive — only archaeological data: tools, settlement remains, cave paintings, and genetic reconstructions.

1.1 Origin of the Xha'ri

The first anatomically modern Xha'ri appeared ~15,000 years ago on the Central Plain of Kharak. Genetic studies indicate that the founder population numbered no more than 5,000–8,000 individuals.

1.1.1 Migrations

Period Route Duration Population
−15,000 – −14,000 Central Plain → Thar coast ~1,000 years 8,000 → 20,000
−14,000 – −12,000 Kharak → northern taiga ~2,000 years 20,000 → 60,000
−12,000 – −11,000 Kharak → Rhuval (via isthmus) ~1,000 years 60,000 → 120,000
−11,000 – −10,000 Rhuval → Vexar (by sea) ~1,000 years 120,000 → 200,000

1.1.2 Early Sites

The oldest archaeological sites:

Site Age (y.a.) Location Finds
Thar-1 ≈14,500 Thar Valley Bone tools, hearth
Mor-Ven ≈13,800 Central Plain Stone axes, Ghal-Shar bones
Khal-Tor ≈12,500 Khal-Tor Island Fishing hooks, boats
Ven-Shar ≈11,200 Southern Vexar Cave paintings

1.2 Way of Life

1.2.1 Economy

Prehistoric Xha'ri were hunter-gatherers with elements of proto-agriculture.

Period Economy Tools
−15,000 – −13,000 Hunting + gathering Stone knives, spears
−13,000 – −11,000 Fishing + hunting Harpoons, traps
−11,000 – −9,800 Proto-agriculture + domestication Hoes, baskets

1.2.2 Social Structure

Clan community (20–50 individuals). Division of labor by age, not by sex — all three Xha'ri sexes participated in hunting and gathering equally.

1.2.3 Spiritual Culture

Cave paintings in the Vexar caves (Ven-Shar site) depict:

  • Three-eyed figures (Xha'ri)
  • Animals (Ghal-Shar, Shar-Ghar)
  • Astronomical symbols (configurations of Serrin and Thel)

1.3 Domestication of the Kharru

~8,000 years ago (by some accounts, earlier) the first animal was domesticated — the kharru (species Xa-ruunidae).

Stage Time Description
Wild hunting −15,000 – −10,000 Primary hunting target
Taming −10,000 – −9,000 Cubs in settlements
Semi-domestication −9,000 – −8,000 Controlled grazing
Full domestication −8,000 Captive breeding

1.4 Archaeological Sites

Key excavated settlements of the prehistoric era:

Site Age Area Population Finds
Thar-1 14,500 years 2 ha ~80 people Tools, hearths
Ven-Shar 11,200 years 12 ha ~500 people Cave paintings, sanctuary
Khal-Tor 12,500 years 8 ha ~300 people Fishing nets, boats

Chapter Summary

  1. The first Xha'ri appeared ~15,000 years ago on the Central Plain of Kharak.
  2. By −9,800 the population had reached ~200,000, spreading across three continents.
  3. Domestication of the kharru — a key event of the prehistoric era.
  4. The cave paintings of Vexar — the earliest evidence of spiritual culture.
  5. The clan community (20–50 individuals) — the basic social unit.
