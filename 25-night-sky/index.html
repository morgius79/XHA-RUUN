Chapter 25: Night Sky of Théxar

"When two moons rise over the plains of Kharak, every Xha'Ruun knows: the sky speaks to us. Serrin — full, round, silver — reminds us of the order of the universe. Thel — swift, reddish, uneven — of the chaos from which we were born." — "Astronomy for Everyone," +3,200 Y.E.

Related sections: → see [Ch.24, "Satellites"], → see [Ch.02, "Star System"], → see [Ch.32, "Khar'Vex System"] Volume: 25 pages Key principle: Théxar's night sky — two satellites, five planets visible to the naked eye, 50 constellations. Due to different diurnal cycles (27.3 h) and year (412.7 days), Xha'Ruun astronomy has unique systems of time measurement and navigation.

25.1.2 Serrin and Thel in the Sky

Serrin is the dominant night object. Its full phase provides illumination of ~0.3 lux (enough for reading). Movement across the sky — from east to west (like other objects, due to Théxar's rotation).

Thel appears as a reddish star with a noticeable angular diameter. Due to its high eccentricity (0.18), its size and brightness change by 25% over the orbital period.

25.2 Starry Sky

25.2.1 Pole Star

Théxar's rotation axis points toward the star Vokh-Ven ("Truth of Harmony"), the pole star. Located in the constellation El-Ven (Truth). The star is a yellow supergiant of spectral class G5Ib, apparent magnitude +2.1.

25.2.3 Milky Way

The galaxy is visible from Théxar at an angle — the galactic plane is inclined 42° to the equatorial plane. This provides a better view of the galactic center for the southern hemisphere.

25.3 Planets in the Sky

25.3.1 Vax (inner planet)

Vax is a bright "morning/evening star." Maximum elongation — 38°. Visible ~2.5 hours after sunset or before sunrise.

25.3.2 Xhel

The second inner planet. Visible to the naked eye as a bluish star. Elongation — up to 52°. Visibility period — up to 4 hours.

25.3.3 Zhal

A mini-Neptune, visible only through a telescope or under ideal conditions. Moves slowly across the sky — remains in one constellation for up to 8 months.

25.5 Astronomy in Xha'Ruun Culture

25.5.1 Calendar

Théxar's astronomical cycles formed the basis of the calendar:

  • Day: 27.3 hours (rotation)
  • Month: 31.4 days (Serrin orbit)
  • Year: 412.7 days (Théxar orbit)
  • Thel cycle: 78.2 days (used for long-term planning)
  • Era of Unity: from +850 Y.E. (founding of the Council of Unity)

25.5.2 Navigation

Stellar navigation on Théxar is highly developed. Main methods:

  • Altitude of Vokh-Ven (Polaris) — determining latitude
  • Position of Khar'Vex at noon — determining longitude
  • Phase of Serrin — determining the day of the month
  • Astrometric instruments — sextants, astrolabes
