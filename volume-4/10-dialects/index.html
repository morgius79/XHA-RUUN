Volume IV: Language — Chapter 10: Dialects

"Three continents — three accents. Kharak — a hard 'r', Rhuval — a melodic 'a', Vexar — a breathy 'h'. The language is one, but the voices are different." — Phonetic Atlas, +3,200 Y.E.

Related sections: → see [Volume IV, Ch.1, "Phonetics"] Length: 20 pages

10.1 Main Dialects

Dialect Continent Speakers Distinguishing Features
Kharak-Thar Kharak 1.2 billion Standard
Kharak-Tor Kharak 0.6 billion Hard "g," glottal stop
Rhuval-Ven Rhuval 0.8 billion Melodic "a," elongated vowels
Vexar-Shan Vexar 0.4 billion Breathy "h," nasal vowels
Island Islands 0.3 billion Archaic vocabulary

10.2 Mutual Intelligibility

All dialects are mutually intelligible. Differences are at the level of pronunciation and up to 5% of vocabulary.
