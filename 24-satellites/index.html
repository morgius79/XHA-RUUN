Chapter 24: Satellites of Théxar — Serrin and Thel

"Two moons — two eyes of the night. Serrin — wise, full, looking forward. Thel — swift, wild, looking backward. Together they illuminate our darkness." — From "Song of Two Moons," Xha'Ruun folk poetry

Related sections: → see [Ch.03, "Planet Théxar"], → see [Ch.16, "Magnetosphere"], → see [Ch.19, "Resources"] Volume: 25 pages Key principle: Théxar has two satellites: Serrin (86.4 мо, regular orbit) and Thel (36.2 мо, captured C-type asteroid). Serrin — stabilizer of the planet's axis and source of tides; Thel — main source of He-3 for fusion energy.

24.1 Serrin

The largest satellite of Théxar. Probably formed simultaneously with the planet from the same protoplanetary disk (co-accretion hypothesis), rather than as a result of a collision.

24.1.3 Surface

Serrin is a typical silicate body covered in regolith. Basalt plains (maria) occupy ~35% of the visible side. The largest crater — Ven-Khal (diameter 36.6 мо), located on the far side.

  • Serrin Maria: 4 large basalt basins
  • Craters: >10,000 with diameter >0.20 мо
  • Surface age: 4.2–3.5 billion cycles

24.2 Thel

Théxar's second satellite — a captured C-type (carbonaceous) asteroid, captured by the planet's gravity ~2.5 billion cycles ago.

24.2.3 He-3 — Strategic Resource

Thel is the main source of helium-3 for Xha'Ruun fusion energy.

Parameter Value
He-3 concentration in regolith 0.002–0.005% (by mass)
Total reserves ~60.6 million кх
Annual extraction ~485,000 кх
Availability ~100,000 years (at current level)
Extraction cost 2.1 cents/kWh (final)

Extraction is carried out by automated stations on Thel's surface. Regolith is heated to 425.7°X; the released He-3 is collected, liquefied, and sent to Théxar.

24.3 Tidal System

The combined influence of Serrin and Thel creates a complex tidal regime.

Period (primary) 12.8 h (semidiurnal) 39.1 h (diurnal) Complex

Tides on Théxar have an average height of 1.2 ша, due to the small mass of the satellites.

24.3.1 Tidal Deceleration

Serrin is gradually moving away from Théxar at a rate of ~1.4 па/year. Théxar's rotation slowdown is ~0.002 s per century.

24.4 Phases and Eclipses

24.4.1 Phases of Serrin

The full cycle of Serrin's phases is 31.4 days. Due to synchronous rotation, one side is always visible from Théxar.

24.4.2 Solar Eclipses

Total solar eclipses occur ~8 times per year, when Serrin passes between Théxar and Khar'Vex. Duration of totality — up to 4 minutes.

24.4.3 Lunar Eclipses

Occur ~12 times per year, when Serrin enters Théxar's shadow. Duration — up to 2 hours.

24.5.1 Serrin-1 Base

Located in the Ven-Khal crater (36.6 мо, far side). Scientific station, telescopes, He-3 mining control center on Thel.

24.5.2 Thel Mining Complex

Automated complex on Thel's surface. Area — 4.96 мо². Consists of 24 drilling stations, 4 processing plants, and a spaceport for sending He-3 to Théxar.
