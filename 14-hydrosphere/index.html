Chapter 14: Oceans of Théxar

"Water is the blood of Théxar. The oceans are its heart, the rivers are its arteries, the clouds are its lungs. As long as the water moves, the planet is alive." — From the "Book of the Cycle", sacred text of Xha'Ruun

Related sections: → see [Ch.03, "Planet Théxar"], → see [Ch.12, "Geological Evolution"], → see [Ch.13, "Continents"] Volume: 35 pages Key principle: 67% of Théxar's surface is covered by water, forming a global hydrological system — two oceans, five seas, an extensive river network. This system determines the climate, provides resources, and is the habitat of a second intelligent species — the Moryn.

Introduction

Water is a fundamental factor of Théxar's habitability. 67% of the planet's surface is covered by ocean — with one important distinction: Théxar has two oceans, not one global one. The system of continents and islands separating them creates complex circulation, unique to each water area.

Théxar's hydrosphere is not simply "water around land." It is a complex dynamic system, including:

  • Two oceans with different circulation and salinity regimes
  • Five seas — each with its own hydrological profile
  • An extensive river network with a total length of ~244,000 mo
  • The cryosphere — polar ice caps covering 0.34 million mo²
  • Subglacial lakes at the south pole (at least 4 major ones)
  • A system of hydrothermal vents on the ocean floor

In this chapter we examine the oceans and seas of Théxar — their physical parameters, circulation, ecosystems, and significance for civilization. Rivers, lakes, and the cryosphere are covered separately. → see [Ch.XX, "Rivers and Lakes"]

Ocean Thalas is the largest body of water on the planet, occupying most of the Northern Hemisphere. Its name comes from the ancient root thal- ("deep", "depth"). The average temperature is higher than that of the southern ocean, due to:

  • A larger share of solar energy reaching the Northern Hemisphere (due to orbital eccentricity)
  • A system of warm currents transporting equatorial heat to the pole
  • The configuration of continents, creating a heat "reservoir" effect between Kharak and Rhuval

Main currents of Thalas:

  • Tor Current (warm) — originates in the equatorial zone off the southern coast of Kharak, moves northwest along the continent, transporting ~32 Sv (1 Sv = Sverdrup, 10⁶ m³/s). Thanks to this current, the northern regions of Kharak are 5.4–8.2°X warmer than would be expected at their latitude.
  • Thalas Current (cold) — return current along the western coast of Rhuval, carrying cold waters from southern latitudes toward the equator.
  • Thalas Circumpolar Current — circles the northern polar cap clockwise.

Ocean Gharra is the southern ocean of Théxar. Its name is linked to the impact basin of the Gharra Sea, formed ~5.5 billion cycles ago. → see [Ch.12, "Planet Formation"]

14.2.1 Deepest Point

Thash Trench (12,010 sha) — the deepest point on the planet, located at the boundary of the Rhuval and Gharra plates in a subduction zone.

At the bottom of the Thash Trench, a unique isolated ecosystem based on chemosynthesis exists. The pressure here reaches ~952 tyazh, water temperature — 46.6°X (1.6°X higher than in similar deep-sea trenches, due to geothermal flow).

14.2.2 Circulation

The circulation of Ocean Gharra is determined by two factors: strong wind forcing ("roaring forties" of the southern latitudes) and a deep-water current from Thalas through the Ven-Khal Strait.

  • Gharra Circumpolar Current — a powerful current circling Vexar from the south. Creates a ring barrier, partially isolating the southern waters.
  • Ven-Khal Current — a cold deep-water current connecting Thalas and Gharra through the strait between Rhuval and Vexar.
  • Deep-water upwelling — off the western coast of Vexar, caused by diverging currents. Provides upwelling of nutrient-rich deep waters.

14.3 Seas

The five seas of Théxar are partially isolated water areas with their own hydrological regimes.

Sea Serrin

The warmest and most productive sea of Théxar. Located between the southern coast of Kharak and the northern coast of Rhuval. Average surface temperature 58.1°X. The sea is named after the planet's largest satellite — Serrin, whose gravitational influence determines the tides.

The biological productivity of the Serrin Sea is the highest on the planet (≈320 g C/m²/year), due to:

  • Constant inflow of nutrients from Kharak's rivers (especially Thar)
  • A well-developed shelf (average depth 1,463 sha)
  • Stable upwelling along both coasts
  • Optimal temperature regime (57.0–59.2°X)

Sea Irkhen

A stormy sea between Kharak and Rhuval, named after the Irkhen Archipelago. Characterized by strong tidal currents (up to 67 sha/mig in straits) and frequent storms (more than 60 days per year with waves >3.7 sha). The most important maritime trade routes connecting Kharak and Rhuval are located here.

Sea Ven-Khal

The only sea connecting two oceans. The Ven-Khal Strait (85 mo at its narrowest point) is the planet's most important maritime corridor. Water exchange between Thalas and Gharra occurs through it, making its hydrological regime transitional.

KEY FACT

Approximately 8% of the total water exchange between Théxar's oceans passes through the Ven-Khal Strait. This water exchange is critically important for the planet's thermohaline circulation.

14.4 Ocean Currents and Thermohaline Circulation

14.4.1 Global Conveyor

As with all habitable worlds, Théxar has a global thermohaline circulation — an "oceanic conveyor" redistributing heat between the equator and the poles.

       GLOBAL THERMOHALINE CIRCULATION

    Thalas (north) ──────────────────────────────
         │                                         │
         │  Warm surface current (Tor)            │
         ▼                                         │
    Equator ←───────────────────────────           │
         │                           │            │
         │  Cold deep current        │            │
         ▼  (Ven-Khal)               │            │
    Gharra (south) ──────────────────╯            │
         │                                         │
         │  Gharra Circumpolar Current             │
         ▼                                         │
    Southern Polar Circle ────────────────────────╯

Fig. 14.2. Diagram of Théxar's global thermohaline circulation.

Circulation stages:

  1. Warm water on the surface of Thalas moves north (Tor Current), releasing heat to the atmosphere
  2. Cooled, denser water sinks in the northern latitudes of Thalas (deep convection)
  3. The deep current moves south, passing through the Ven-Khal Strait into Ocean Gharra
  4. In Ocean Gharra, part of the deep water rises (upwelling), closing the cycle

Parameters of Théxar's thermohaline circulation:

Parameter Value
Deep convection zone Thalas (30°–50°N)
Average speed (deep) 10.2 pa/mig
Full cycle ~2,500 years
Heat transport ~0.8 PW
Deep T 46.6°X

Théxar's slower circulation (full cycle 2.5× longer) is a consequence of weaker wind forcing and a smaller salinity gradient between water areas.

14.4.2 Tides

Théxar's tidal system is determined by two satellites — Serrin and Thel:

Parameter Serrin influence Thel influence Combined

Tidal height (max) 2.2 sha 0.4 sha 2.6 sha
Tidal height (avg) 1.1 sha 0.1 sha 1.2 sha

The average tidal height on Théxar is 1.2 sha, due to the smaller mass and greater distance of the main satellites.

Hadal 8,537+ Complete darkness Extremophiles (trenches)

14.5.2 Coral Reefs

Théxar's coral ecosystems differ in two key parameters:

  • Biomineralization: instead of calcite (CaCO₃), a silicon-calcium composite is used, more resistant to dissolution in the slightly acidic environment of Ocean Gharra (pH 7.8)
  • Symbiosis: reef-building organisms enter symbiosis not only with photosynthesizing algae but also with chemoautotrophic bacteria, allowing reefs to thrive at greater depths

The largest reef system is the Great Thalas Reef (extending ~650 mo along the southern coast of Kharak).

14.5.3 Hydrothermal Vents

At least 12 hydrothermal vent fields ("black smokers") have been discovered on the floor of both Théxar's oceans:

Field Ocean Depth (sha) Vent T (°X) Unique feature
Tor-1 Thalas 3,902 232.6 Largest field (0.33 mo²)
Thash-Deep Gharra 10,000 254.3 Deepest field on the planet
Gharra-Vent Gharra 5,854 213.5 Rich in sulfur and rare-earth elements

Vent ecosystems are based on H₂S chemosynthesis — using a 6-letter genetic code, which provides significantly greater biochemical diversity. → see [Ch.04, "Biochemistry"]

14.6.1 Deep-Sea Mining

Théxar is one of the few known civilizations to have developed industrial deep-sea mining of polymetallic nodules at depths up to 7,317 sha. Extraction is carried out by specialized automatic stations using bio-optical computers for navigation in conditions of complete darkness and high pressure.

14.7.2 Moryn — Oceanic Intelligence

Théxar's oceans are the habitat of the Moryn, the planet's second intelligent species. → see [Ch.05, "Intelligent Species"]

The Moryn primarily inhabit the deep-water zones of Ocean Gharra, where pressure and temperature are stable. Their civilization is built on bioluminescent communication, hydrothermal energy, and cultivation of deep-sea ecosystems. Contact between the Moryn and the Xha'ri is conducted through special interface stations at depths of 244–610 sha.

14.7.3 Law of Silence and Ocean Protection

In accordance with the Law of Silence (CANON.md §4.3), certain ocean zones are declared protected. The largest of these — the Gharra Deep protected zone (0.51 million mo²) — covers the deep-water trenches of the southern ocean, including the habitat of the Moryn. Any industrial activity is prohibited in this zone, and scientific research is strictly regulated.

Chapter Summary

  1. Théxar has two oceans: Thalas (3.9 million mo²) and Gharra (3.6 million mo²), separated by continents.
  2. The planet's deepest point is Thash Trench (12,010 sha) in Ocean Gharra.
  3. Five seas have unique hydrological regimes — from the warm Serrin to the polar Koss.
  4. Thermohaline circulation is 2.5× slower (full cycle ~2,500 years).
  5. Marine ecosystems include unique silicon-calcium reefs and deep-water chemosynthesizing communities.
  6. The Moryn — an intelligent species inhabiting the deep-water zones of Ocean Gharra.
  7. Ocean energy provides ~83 TWh/year (~8% of the planet's energy balance).
